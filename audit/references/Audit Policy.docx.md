**Audit Policy**

1. **Overview**

The Audit Policy enables and charges \<BANK\> to perform regular reviews of controls. To confirm that effective controls have been implemented and to evaluate risk management practices, a variety of audit types are necessary and results must be effectively presented to management.

2. **Purpose**

The purpose of the Audit Policy is to regularly test the key controls, systems, and procedures of the information security program. To establish the frequency and objective of such tests based on risk assessment results. The Audit Policy also ensures tests are conducted or reviewed by independent third parties or staff independent of those that develop or maintain the security programs.

3. **Responsibilities**  
   1. **Board of Directors**

The Board of Directors is responsible for establishing an Audit Committee to oversee the audit function. The Board of Directors is also responsible for the review of testing results to ensure that sufficient resources are invested to implement and test the approved controls. This policy shall be reviewed and approved by the Board of Directors at least annually.

2. **Audit Committee**

\<BANK\> shall designate an Audit Committee to oversee the audit function. The Audit Committee shall meet monthly to fulfill this requirement. The Audit Committee shall have the following specific responsibilities at a minimum:

* Develop and manage the Audit Program  
* Approve audit frequencies and schedules  
* Approve audit objectives  
* Approve the scope of audits  
* Engage external auditors / authorize execution of an audit  
* Promptly review and approve audit reports  
* Deliver audit results to the Board of Directors  
* Oversee responses to audit reports  
  3. **Internal Auditor**

The Internal Auditor is responsible for conducting audits according to the scope and schedule approved by the Audit Committee. The Internal Auditor is also responsible for the timely reporting of audit findings and recommendations.

4. **Audit Types**

Various audit types shall be conducted to evaluate risk management practices, internal control systems, and compliance with documented policies or standards. Each audit shall be risk focused, promote sound IT controls, and inform the Board of Directors of the effectiveness of risk management practices. To enhance audit effectiveness, follow-up and corrective actions to prior audits or regulatory examinations shall be utilized when possible. At a minimum, the following types of audits shall be executed at least annually.

* **Administrative Audit:** The purpose of an administrative audit is to verify that current processes conform to documented policies and supporting documents implemented by management. Documented requirements in policies and supporting documents are identified and evaluated using interview, physical observation, and documentation review to determine if management is adequately implementing and enforcing them.  
* **Baseline Compliance Audit:** The purpose of a baseline compliance audit is to verify that processes and documented policies conform to regulations, standards, or other baselines. Applicable requirements in regulations, standards, or baselines are identified and the processes and policies implemented by management are evaluated using interviews, physical observations, and documentation reviews to determine if controls are present.  
* **Social Engineering Assessment:** The purpose of a social engineering assessment is to verify that employees can recognize social engineering attacks and are trained on how to properly respond to them. Unauthorized individuals attempt to gain access to protected information by using various forms of social engineering attacks.  
* **Acceptable Use Assessment:** The purpose of an acceptable use assessment is to verify that employees are aware of and comply with the Acceptable Use Policy, Confidentiality Agreement, and other applicable documents and agreements. This specialized type of administrative audit uses specialized scanning tools and other methods to evaluate the documented requirements in the acceptable use policy and other applicable documents and agreements.  
5. **Network Assessments**

In addition to performing audits to evaluate risk management practices, internal control systems, and compliance with documented policies or standards. \<BANK\> shall perform and/or contract for the performance of the following technical assessments on internal and external IT systems at least annually.

* **Vulnerability Assessment:** The purpose of a vulnerability assessment is to verify that system and application configurations and patching efforts are operational and effective. Known vulnerabilities are identified on internal IT systems by using specialized scanning tools.  
* **Penetration Test:** The purpose of a penetration test is to verify that system and network configurations are operational and effective. IT systems and networks are probed for vulnerabilities by using specialized scanning tools.  
* **System Configuration Assessment:** The purpose of a system configuration assessment is to verify that systems conform to an industry accepted standard or baseline. This specialized type of compliance audit uses specialized scanning tools and physical observations to evaluate if the system and operational processes meet documented requirements.  
6. **Audit Reporting**

A report of audit results shall be documented and presented to management for review. Each audit report shall include the following:

* A description of the audit scope and objective  
* Applicable dates of coverage  
* A description of findings and deficiencies  
* Recommendations for corrective actions

To ensure audit results are addressed, a method for tracking responses, corrective actions, and outstanding deficiencies shall be documented and managed for each audit.

Additionally, to provide clear support for audit results and work performed, audit work papers shall also be documented and made available. Audit work papers shall illustrate the procedures followed to carry out the audit, complete with audit findings, comments, and recommendations. Audit work papers shall address all areas in the scope of the audit and contain sufficient evidence of the tasks performed and support any conclusions reached.
