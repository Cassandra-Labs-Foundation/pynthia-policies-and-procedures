### OVERVIEW

In response to the final regulations interpreting the identity theft red flag provisions of the FACT Act effective January 1, 2008, each financial institution or creditor must develop and implement a written identity theft prevention program. This program is required to detect, prevent, and mitigate identity theft in connection with the opening of a covered account or any existing covered account. The program must be appropriate to the size and complexity of the financial institution or creditor.

Customer confidentiality and information security are top priorities. The bank’s identity theft program consists of a combination of specific policies and procedures designed to safeguard customers’ personal information. The Bank also holds its vendors to the same level of accountability. 

**COVERED ACCOUNTS**

A *Covered Account* is an account that a financial institution or creditor offers or maintains primarily for personal, family, or household purposes, that involves multiple payments or transactions, as well as any other account for which there is a reasonably foreseeable risk to customers or to the safety and soundness of the financial institution or creditor from identity theft.

**ELEMENTS OF THE PROGRAM**

The program must include reasonable policies and procedures to:

•	***Identify*** relevant red flags and incorporate those red flags into the program.  
•	***Detect*** red flags incorporated into the program.  
•	***Respond*** appropriately to any detected red flags in order to prevent and mitigate 	identity theft.  
•	Ensure that the ***program is updated periodically*** to reflect changes in risks to 	customers and the safety and soundness of the financial institution or creditor.

Above all, the program needs to be effective and flexible in identifying and responding to potential red flag situations regardless of the degree of complexity. The effectiveness of the program is the overriding element and the basis of which it will be examined.

**ADMINISTRATION OF THE PROGRAM**

Each financial institution or creditor that is required to implement a program must provide for its continued administration and must:  
•	Obtain approval of the initial written program from its Board of Directors or an 	appropriate committee of the board.  
•	Involve the board, an appropriate board committee, or a designated member of senior management in the oversight, development, and implementation of the program.  
•	Train staff as necessary to effectively implement the program.  
•	Exercise appropriate and effective oversight of service provider arrangements.

Annually, the Compliance Manager reports on compliance with this Regulation to the Vice President of Operations. The report addresses matters related to the ID Theft Prevention Program and should evaluate issues such as:

* Effectiveness of the policies and procedures in addressing the risk of identity theft;  
* Significant incidents involving identity theft and management’s response;  
* Recommendations for changes to the Program; and  
* Service provider arrangements.

**IDENTIFYING RELEVANT RED FLAGS**

As required by the Regulation, an inventory and risk assessment has been completed to analyze those red flags (see separate attachment), as well as any red flags unique to our institution. In completing the identification and risk ratings, a variety of factors were considered:

\-Types of accounts: The types of covered accounts we offer or maintain for our customers.  
\-Methods to open accounts: The methods we provide to customers to open our covered accounts.  
\-Methods of access: The methods we provide our customers to access their covered accounts.  
\-Any prior identity theft experience: Past incidents of identity theft.

The risk ratings of inherent risk and residual risk were used. For our purpose, inherent risk was defined as the risk linked to the activity itself. For example, checking accounts would have more inherent risk due to the number and variety of transactions allowed versus a savings account, limited by Reg. D restrictions. Residual risk was defined as the risk remaining after the activity was filtered through appropriate control measures. The risk rating itself can be considered quite subjective in nature but was based on the nature of the activity and characteristics of each account type. 

The red flag examples listed in appendix J provided a sufficient and relevant selection for Sound Community Bank, and no unique red flags were identified.

**DETECTING RED FLAGS** 

Sound Community Bank has adequate procedures in place to protect the institution and consumers against identity left. These procedures involve several elements including the Metavante host deposit system, internal policies and procedures, as well as, outside vendors integrated for consumer reporting. 

**New Deposit and Transaction Accounts**

The USA Patriot Act requires all financial institutions to verify the identity of every person who seeks to open an account, or become a signer on an account with the institution after October 25, 2002\. All clients are subject to the identity verification even though they may be a long term customer and well known to us. An account includes every formal banking relationship. Examples include deposit accounts, loans, and safe deposit box rentals.

The account opening process represents the largest opportunity to allow unauthorized use of an individual’s personal information to establish erroneous accounts. Therefore, several steps are taken when requesting and verifying the personal information of the individual requesting the account.

Prior to establishing any new account, SCB requires valid and unexpired identification from all persons requesting account access. Our Customer Identification Policy included in the *BSA/AML Operations Bulletin (OB \#06-012)*, specifically outlines the identification requirements and examples of ineligible clients.

New account relationships are established using the Metavante MIDO platform. The MIDO application consists of several screens completed at account inception by bank personnel. These screens gather a variety of data including customer identifying information, due diligence, and demographic information to be used for risk rating and marketing purposes. Signature cards, disclosures, and authorizations are generated through this system.

The MIDO account origination system includes an electronic signature pad allowing quick access by bank personnel to verify signatures when needed. This system is also important in verifying signatures of incoming items such as wire transfers and mail requests.  

For a complete outline of Account Opening Process, please see the *New Accounts Ownership Guide (8/08)* in Sound Info.

All new clients must be screened using Decision Power and Chexsystems.  The Decision Power inquiry will perform the OFAC verification, match name and social security number, and generate Safescan warnings and a full credit report. The Chexsystems database is automatically selected as part of the new account opening process. Together, these databases help us detect fraud and prevent losses. Existing clients who request a checking account will also be screened through Chexsystems. 

Please refer to *Loan Bulletin (LB \#02-007)* for complete information on Decision Power.

**New Loan Requests**

For the purposes of complying with the USA Patriot Act, lending staff ensures that appropriate identification is obtained for every new loan for which we have not obtained acceptable identification in accordance with the USA Patriot Act. This will most likely be required of:

1\. Existing customers whose accounts were opened prior to 10/25/02.  
2\. New loan customers who are not opening a deposit account.

If there is doubt that acceptable identification was obtained at the time of opening an account, it is appropriate to obtain identification at the time the loan closes. Identification is only required after the loan has been approved, and documents are prepared for signing. In no event is the identification to be obtained prior to loan approval, or be used in the decision making process. *Loan Bulletin (LB \#04-003)* contains an Identification Form for use in gathering identification. 

**Existing Accounts**

Existing accounts pose unique problems as they are already part of the bank’s database. This allows the potential for unauthorized access to a current customer’s account information, as a channel has been created. Special care needs to be taken to ensure bank personnel are conducting business with the actual customer.

Accounts can be accessed through various means: 

* In person  
* Telephone (both live and telebanking)   
* Online Banking   
* ATM (using debit card)  
* In writing

Multifactor authentication procedures have been adopted and integrated into the banks access channels. The following sections describe procedures for each method of contact.

In person contact requires a minimum of 1 piece of valid unexpired ID as well as verification of one or more unique identifying factors. Live telephone contact requires verification of at least 2 unique factors with the caller, which can include a password designated by the customer. A detailed outline of procedures for both live and telephonic customer contact can be found in *Operations Bulletin (OB \#06-010).*

The telebanking system can be accessed only if the customer knows their account number and inputs the correct personal identification number. The customer is responsible for determining their access code. After three unsuccessful tries, the system locks out further attempts and directs the caller to phone the institution. 

Online Banking and Bill-Payer services are provided through Metavante. Customers register their accounts utilizing unique information, including but not limited to: last statement balance or debit card PIN number.  IP address registration may be accomplished with the assignment of a password, picture recognition, and cookie placement. This method of multifactor authentication has proven an effective method of safeguarding customers’ confidential account information. Temporary one-time access can be allowed if the customer needs to sign in from an unregistered pc. This can be enabled only after specific predetermined questions have been answered correctly. 

Debit cards serve a dual function, as a point of sale currency medium, as well as allowing access to the customer’s account through the ATM network. Cards and PINs are mailed under separate cover when newly issued. The bank can facilitate a customer’s request for a replacement PIN through the host system.

Incoming written requests for actions such as check withdrawals, address changes, account closures, and certificate renewals are centrally received at the Client Service Center, and then routed to the appropriate branch for completion. Branch personnel take steps to make direct contact with the customer prior to completing the request.

**Credit Report Warnings/Alerts**

The FACT Act establishes three types of fraud alerts:  
**Initial fraud alert**: The- consumer asserts in good faith a suspicion of ID Theft. This alert is in place for no less than ninety days.  
**Extended fraud alert**: This alert is established for a 7 year period and the ID theft has been reported to local police or FTC  
**Active duty alert**: This alert is limited to individuals in the military and indicates that the consumer is assigned to service away from the usual duty station. For instance the consumer may be overseas.

Upon receipt of a consumer report that contains an initial, extended, or active duty alert, it is the responsibility of the employee to re-verify the identity of the customer, utilizing the established CIP procedures. In addition, at least one additional piece of identification is required. If the alert contains instructions to contact the consumer before taking any action on the request, the employee must contact the consumer in the manner specified to verify identity. Finally, the employee must note the documentation on the credit report and loan set up sheet with the additional verification and client contact.

Refer to *Loan Bulletin (LB \#06-004)* for further information on Fraud Alert procedures.

**Credit Report Error Resolution Resulting From Identity Theft**

As a provider of consumer credit information, Sound Community Bank has a responsibility to report accurate information. Additionally, the Bank is responsible for investigating any dispute regarding the information reported to the credit bureau. If it has been determined that the loan is a result of identity theft, the loan will be flagged on our system as “identity theft” and charged off the system. To ensure this debt is not reported to a credit reporting agency, we will code the account appropriately.

For further details on credit report error resolution, please see *Loan Bulletin (LB \#06-006).* 

**Address Changes**

Maintaining current and correct addresses for our clients is critical for the prevention of identity theft and for appropriate customer service. This includes all types of accounts. Specific procedures have been developed to ensure address changes are only completed after thoroughly identifying the individual. In person requests are performed only after photo identification is produced, and phone requests are completed only after appropriate second-level identification is performed (verification of last deposit, collateral held on a loan, etc.).

Special handling of address changes coupled with debit card reissues within 30 days are in place-as required by the Act. The Operations Department reviews a report of all address changes performed 30 days prior to a debit card order for validation.

For a complete description of address changes, please see the *Operations Manual Account Servicing on Address Changes* in Sound Info.

**Returned Mail/Debit Cards**

Returned mail is a risk factor for identity theft, as well as an internal control threat to a financial institution. The financial institution may or may not be aware of a change of address. Once mail of any type is returned, the item is opened and logged into the returned mail database maintained by the Operations Department. Alerts are placed on the host system warning employees of the need for address verification.

Returned debit cards require special handling. The returned debit card is statused as “Hotcarded” on the host system, rendering the card invalid. The card is shredded and additional notes are placed on the account of the status. Finally, the branch of account is notified of the returned debit card and account status. **Returned debit cards cannot be forwarded. Once the address is updated a new debit card can be opened for the customer**.

For a complete description of returned mail procedures, please see the *Operations Manual Account Servicing on Returned Mail Procedures.*

**Integrity of Customer Information** 

Sound Community Bank currently has a Customer Information Security Breach Response Program as mandated by Federal Banking Regulations. The new guidance helps financial institutions follow standard procedures to help mitigate customer Identity Theft.

This program addresses potential incidents of unauthorized access to customer information such as customer account files, debit card files, or loan files. This is an enhancement of the Information Security Policy previously developed and is aimed at improving security in the handling of sensitive consumer information. 

Sensitive customer information is defined as information that would allow access to a customer’s account or allow someone to adopt a customer’s identity. Sensitive information also includes any combination of information that would allow access to the customer’s account such as online banking user name and password, or debit card number and PIN.

In the event an instance of identity theft or suspicion of identity theft arises during collection efforts, the collection dept. is to notify the Operations Vice President immediately. 

For a complete outline of the Customer Information Security Breach Response Program and steps to follow in the event of an information security breach, please see the section in the *Operations Manual* for the *Customer Information Security Breach Response Program.*
