**Nondeposit Investment Products Policy**

**Purpose**

The Bank is committed to delivering to its customers a full line of appropriate and suitable financial products and services, which includes Nondeposit Investment Products (NDIPs).  A crucial part of this policy is to make clear the distinction between deposit and nondeposit products through the required disclosures and other procedures.

**Objective**

The objective of the offering of NDIPs is to help the Bank’s customers reach their investment goals.  Recognizing that each customer has individual parameters concerning risk, reward, liquidity, taxes and investment horizon, the offering of NDIPs will focus on the needs of the individual customer based on consultative selling.

The Bank’s offering of NDIPs are overseen by \[Title of Individual Responsible\], who is responsible for managing and overseeing this function. This includes compliance with all applicable laws, regulations and guidance. In additional to applicable insurance and securities laws, regulations, and guidance, it also includes those issued by the \[Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, Office of the Comptroller of the Currency, Federal Reserve Board\] such as the FDIC regulations regarding the official advertising statement requirements, the insurance sales rules, and the Interagency Statement on Retail Sales of Nondeposit Investment Products.

NDIP-related transactions will be handled according to an agreement between the Bank and \[NAME OF INSURANCE/BROKERAGE SERVICE PROVIDER\], and a Bank Registered Representative.  By entering into these agreements, the representative has agreed to follow the compliance rules of \[NAME OF INSURANCE/BROKERAGE SERVICE PROVIDER\] and its regulating entities, including rules concerning fair conduct and product suitability.

**NDIP Investment Personnel**

Personnel offering NDIPs, investment representatives, must have the appropriate securities and insurance licenses to deliver the investment-related products deemed necessary by this Bank to meet the customer’s needs, and in accordance with agreements with \[NAME OF INSURANCE/BROKERAGE SERVICE PROVIDER\].

**NDIP Products**

The Bank will provide the following NDIP products:

* \[Fixed annuities\]

* \[Variable annuities\]

* \[Life Insurance\]

* \[Stocks\] 

* \[Bonds\]

* \[Mutual Funds and Unit Investment Trusts (UITs)\]

* \[Certain private offerings with \[NAME OF INSURANCE/BROKERAGE SERVICE PROVIDER\] and \[NAME OF INSTITUTION\] Board approval.\]

Private offerings shall be made available only to investors who meet the state’s suitability requirements and provided that the NDIP representative complies with the State of \[NAME OF STATE\] “Blue Sky Law.”  The private offerings will be reviewed annually by the \[NAME OF INSTITUTION\] Board of Directors.

**NDIP Procedures**

1. NDIP activities will take place in a designated location in the Bank.  That location shall have a sign designating it as the place where NDIPs are offered and carry such disclosures as required by bank, securities and insurance regulations.

2. Margin accounts, options transactions, and initial public offerings of stocks can only be handled subject to prior approval by the \[NAME OF INSURANCE/BROKERAGE SERVICE PROVIDER\].

3. The Bank recognizes that any transaction by the investment representative must meet the client’s investment objectives and is subject to NASD’s rules of suitability.

4. To ensure that customers do not confuse securities and insurance products with FDIC insurance or Bank obligations, each customer will sign a disclosure document at the time of opening a NDIP account or purchasing a separate NDIP product. \[(See copy of \[NAME OF INSURANCE/BROKERAGE SERVICE PROVIDER\]’s disclosure statement.)\]

5. The Bank will keep a file of all customer complaints, written or oral, relating to the NDIP activities.  

6. The investment representative will use stationery and business cards, in the case of non-Bank activities and duties that identify the NDIP representative as a representative of \[NAME OF INSURANCE/BROKERAGE SERVICE PROVIDER\].

7. NDIP advertising shall comply with applicable banking, securities and insurance regulations.  It will clearly identify that its products are not FDIC insured or obligations of the Bank, that there is investment risk and that there is a potential for a loss of principal.  All advertising shall be submitted to and approved by \[NAME OF INSURANCE/BROKERAGE SERVICE PROVIDER\] and the Bank’s compliance officer as appropriate, according to the product advertised.

8. NDIP compensation will be negotiated between the Bank and the investment representative in accordance with bank and securities regulatory requirements. 

9. Referral fees to non-licensed personnel may be paid but only in accordance with current NASD and banking agency rules.

10. The Bank will notify annually all Bank personnel as to its official policy relating to securities transactions through \[NAME OF INSURANCE/BROKERAGE SERVICE PROVIDER\].  \[(See Appendix to this policy.)\]

11. The Bank and \[NAME OF INSURANCE/BROKERAGE SERVICE PROVIDER\] will evaluate the specific product recommendations that the NDIP representative will be marketing and soliciting.  This will be done through review of the product investment performance over an extended period of time as reported by independent sources such as \[Morningstar, Value-Line, A.M. Best, Moody’s, Standard and Poor’s\], and other sources.

12. Quarterly, the Bank compliance officer will review five randomly selected investment transactions.  This review should encompass suitability of recommendations and adequacy of information obtained from the customer, and compliance with Bank policies.  The complaint file will also be reviewed quarterly.
