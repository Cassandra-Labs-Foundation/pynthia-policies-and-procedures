# Internal Controls Policy

## General Policy Statement

Pynthia Credit Union maintains a system of internal controls — segregation of duties, authorization limits, reconciliations, access and change controls, override management, continuous monitoring, and tamper-evident recordkeeping — that safeguards assets, ensures reliable financial reporting, and promotes compliance with applicable laws and regulations. The framework applies to every operational and financial process, business unit, employee, and supporting system across the credit union, including activities performed by third-party service providers on its behalf. Control risk concentrates wherever a single individual could initiate, approve, record, and conceal a transaction, and wherever management lacks timely evidence that controls operate as designed; this policy targets those concentrations directly. Governance is centralized with the Chief Compliance Officer, with the board and Supervisory Committee providing oversight consistent with the Federal Credit Union Act and NCUA regulations.

## IC-01 — Control Environment and Governance {#ic-01-control-environment-and-governance}

- **WHY (Reg cite):** The Federal Credit Union Act, [12 U.S.C. §1761b](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1761b), assigns the Supervisory Committee the duty to ensure internal controls are established and effectively maintained, and [12 CFR §741.3](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/section-741.3) conditions share insurance on sound practices including adequate internal controls. [12 CFR Part 715](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/part-715) requires an internal control structure capable of supporting the annual Supervisory Committee audit.
- **SYSTEM BEHAVIOR:** The credit union maintains a documented, COSO-aligned control framework covering control environment, risk assessment, control activities, information and communication, and monitoring. Every material process has a named control owner recorded in the control register; ownership cannot be vacant for more than 30 days — the CCO is interim owner by default. The board approves the framework and this policy; the Supervisory Committee reviews framework effectiveness and receives monitoring results under [IC-07](#ic-07-monitoring-and-self-assessment). The full framework is re-reviewed and re-approved at least annually. The control register is write-restricted to Compliance; process owners may propose changes but cannot commit them.
- **EVENTS:**

  | When | What's needed | Produced (and logged) | Within |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Annual framework review window opens (`control.framework_review_due`) | Current control register (`control.register`), prior-year deficiency summary (`deficiency.summary`), org/process change log (`process.change_log[]`) | Updated framework and register approved by board (`control.framework_approved`) | 12 months from prior approval (internal: complete 30 days before anniversary; enforced by `control.framework_review_due_at`) |
  | Material process added or changed (`process.created`) | Process description (`process.description`), risk rating (`process.risk_rating`), proposed owner (`process.owner_id`) | Control-owner assignment recorded in register (`control.owner_assigned`) | 30 days (internal: 10 BD) |
  | Control ownership vacated (`control.owner_vacated`) | Vacancy reason (`control.vacancy_reason`), successor candidates (`process.owner_candidates[]`) | Interim CCO ownership then permanent reassignment (`control.owner_assigned`) | 30 days maximum vacancy (enforced by `control.owner_vacancy_timer`) |

- **ALERTS/METRICS:** Alert when any framework review is within 30 days of its 12-month deadline without a scheduled board agenda item; target zero control-register entries with vacant ownership older than 30 days; report percentage of material processes with current owners (target 100%) quarterly.

## IC-02 — Segregation of Duties {#ic-02-segregation-of-duties}

- **WHY (Reg cite):** [12 CFR §741.3(b)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/section-741.3) requires sound internal controls as a condition of insurability, and NCUA/FFIEC examiner guidance under [12 CFR Part 715](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/part-715) treats separation of transaction initiation, authorization, custody, recording, and reconciliation as a baseline expectation for an auditable control structure.
- **SYSTEM BEHAVIOR:** No single individual may control all phases of a transaction — initiation, authorization, custody, recording, and reconciliation. Compliance maintains a machine-readable conflict matrix mapping incompatible duty combinations to roles; the system evaluates the matrix at provisioning time (blocking conflicting entitlement grants) and at runtime (blocking a user from approving or reconciling a transaction they initiated or recorded). Where headcount makes full separation impractical in a small unit, the unit operates under a documented compensating control — mandatory secondary review or job rotation — approved by the CCO and recorded in the control register. Attempted violations are blocked, logged, and routed to Compliance for review. The conflict matrix is write-restricted to Compliance.
- **EVENTS:**

  | When | What's needed | Produced (and logged) | Within |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Entitlement grant requested that would create an incompatible combination (`sod.conflict_detected`) | Requested role/entitlement (`access.entitlement_requested`), user's existing entitlements (`access.entitlements[]`), conflict matrix version (`sod.matrix_version`) | Grant blocked with reason (`sod.grant_blocked`) | Real time (block before grant) |
  | User attempts to act on a transaction in a conflicting capacity, e.g., approving own initiation (`sod.violation_attempted`) | Transaction reference (`transaction.id`), user role (`user.role`), originating actor (`transaction.initiated_by`) | Action blocked and violation record created (`sod.violation_logged`) | Real time block; Compliance review within 2 BD (enforced by `sod.review_timer`) |
  | Compensating control proposed for a unit that cannot fully segregate (`sod.compensating_control_proposed`) | Unit and process (`process.id`), proposed compensating control (`sod.compensating_control`), risk rationale (`sod.risk_rationale`) | CCO-approved compensating control entry in register (`sod.compensating_control_approved`) | Before the unit processes further transactions (internal: 10 BD decision SLA) |

- **ALERTS/METRICS:** Daily count of blocked SoD attempts by unit (investigate any cluster of 3 or more from one user in 30 days); target zero unreviewed violation logs older than 2 business days; quarterly report of active compensating controls with age (flag any older than 12 months for re-justification).

## IC-03 — Authorization and Approval Limits {#ic-03-authorization-and-approval-limits}

- **WHY (Reg cite):** [12 CFR §741.3](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/section-741.3) sound-practices requirements and the Supervisory Committee's verification duties under [12 CFR Part 715](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/part-715) presuppose that transactions are executed only under documented, role-appropriate authority; [12 U.S.C. §1761b](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1761b) makes the adequacy of those authorization controls a Supervisory Committee concern.
- **SYSTEM BEHAVIOR:** Compliance and Finance jointly maintain a role-based authority matrix defining transaction and approval limits by transaction type, amount band, and risk classification. The system enforces the matrix at execution time: a transaction above the initiator's limit cannot execute without a documented approval from a role with sufficient authority, captured before execution. High-risk transactions — including wires above the dual-control threshold, GL manual entries above the defined band, and any transaction touching suspense or clearing accounts — require dual control: two distinct, SoD-compatible approvers per [IC-02](#ic-02-segregation-of-duties). Approvals reference the matrix version in force at approval time so later matrix changes do not retroactively validate or invalidate past decisions. The authority matrix is write-restricted to Compliance, with Finance concurrence required for limit changes.
- **EVENTS:**

  | When | What's needed | Produced (and logged) | Within |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Transaction submitted above initiator's limit (`transaction.approval_required`) | Amount and type (`transaction.amount`, `transaction.type`), initiator role (`user.role`), applicable matrix entry (`authority.matrix_entry`) | Documented approval or rejection (`transaction.approval_recorded`) | Before execution (internal: same BD decision SLA; enforced by `transaction.approval_timer`) |
  | High-risk transaction requires dual control (`transaction.dual_control_required`) | First approval (`transaction.approval_recorded`), second approver eligibility (`access.entitlements[]`), SoD check result (`sod.check_result`) | Second independent approval; transaction released (`transaction.dual_control_completed`) | Before execution |
  | Authority matrix change proposed (`authority.matrix_change_proposed`) | Proposed limits (`authority.proposed_limits[]`), business justification (`authority.change_rationale`), Finance concurrence (`authority.finance_concurrence`) | Versioned matrix update (`authority.matrix_updated`) | Before new limits take effect (internal: 10 BD review SLA) |

- **ALERTS/METRICS:** Target zero transactions executed without a matching approval record; alert on any approval recorded after execution timestamp (out-of-order approval); monthly distribution of approval latency by transaction type; alert when any single approver accounts for more than 80% of a unit's high-risk approvals in a quarter.

## IC-04 — Reconciliations {#ic-04-reconciliations}

- **WHY (Reg cite):** [12 CFR Part 715](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/part-715) requires an internal control structure sufficient to support the annual audit and verification of member accounts, and [12 CFR §741.3](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/section-741.3) requires accurate, current books and records — both of which depend on timely reconciliation of the general ledger, subsidiary ledgers, and suspense/clearing accounts.
- **SYSTEM BEHAVIOR:** Finance reconciles cash, suspense, clearing, and high-volume accounts each business day, and all other general ledger and subsidiary ledger accounts monthly, using the platform reconciliation check (`GET /bookkeeping/reconciliation`) to compare ledger balances (`bookkeeping_entry.running_balance`) against source-system positions, including the Blnk ledger reference (`account.blnk_ledger_id`). Each reconciliation is performed by someone independent of transaction initiation and recording for the accounts concerned, per [IC-02](#ic-02-segregation-of-duties). Reconciling items are aged from first appearance; items open beyond 30 days escalate to the Controller and items open beyond 90 days require formal disposition (correction, write-off under [IC-03](#ic-03-authorization-and-approval-limits) limits, or documented exception under [IC-06](#ic-06-exception-and-override-management)). Reconciliation sign-off records are write-restricted to Finance, with read access for Compliance and Internal Audit.
- **EVENTS:**

  | When | What's needed | Produced (and logged) | Within |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Business-day close for cash/suspense/clearing/high-volume accounts (`recon.daily_due`) | Ledger entries (`bookkeeping_entry.running_balance`), source balances (`account.balances.balance`), Blnk reference (`account.blnk_ledger_id`) | Signed daily reconciliation with item list (`recon.daily_completed`) | Next business day 12:00 (enforced by `recon.daily_timer`) |
  | Month-end close for all other GL/subsidiary accounts (`recon.monthly_due`) | GL trial balance (`gl.trial_balance`), subsidiary ledger totals (`ledger.subsidiary_totals[]`), 5300 mapping (`bookkeeping.account_code_5300`) | Signed monthly reconciliation pack (`recon.monthly_completed`) | 10 BD after month-end (enforced by `recon.monthly_timer`) |
  | Reconciling item passes aging threshold (`recon.item_aged`) | Item detail (`recon.item`), age (`recon.item_age_days`), responsible owner (`recon.item_owner`) | Escalation to Controller; disposition decision (`recon.item_escalated`, then `recon.item_resolved`) | Escalate at 30 days; disposition by 90 days (enforced by `recon.item_aging_timer`) |

- **ALERTS/METRICS:** Alert on any daily reconciliation not completed by 12:00 next business day; aging report of open reconciling items with counts at 30/60/90-day buckets (target zero items past 90 days without formal disposition); trend of unreconciled dollar value by account category month over month.

## IC-05 — Access and Change Controls {#ic-05-access-and-change-controls}

- **WHY (Reg cite):** [12 CFR Part 748](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/part-748) requires a security program protecting records and member information, and the internal control structure required by [12 CFR Part 715](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/part-715) depends on access being limited to those with a business need and on financial systems changing only under documented approval.
- **SYSTEM BEHAVIOR:** System access to financial applications and control configurations is provisioned on least-privilege and need-to-know principles, validated against the SoD conflict matrix in [IC-02](#ic-02-segregation-of-duties) at grant time. Managers and Compliance review entitlements at least annually and on every role change; access for terminated or transferred employees is revoked or re-baselined the same day HR records the status change. Changes to financial systems and control configurations — including the authority matrix, conflict matrix, reconciliation rules, and override thresholds — pass through change-management approval with a documented requester, approver (distinct from the implementer), test evidence, and rollback plan; emergency changes are permitted with post-implementation approval within 2 business days. This control governs the administrative process around access; technical logical-access controls (authentication standards, network security) are out of scope and covered by the Information Security Policy. Entitlement and change records are write-restricted to the Access Administration and Change Management functions respectively.
- **EVENTS:**

  | When | What's needed | Produced (and logged) | Within |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Access requested for a financial system (`access.requested`) | Role and justification (`access.justification`), manager approval (`access.manager_approval`), SoD check result (`sod.check_result`) | Provisioned entitlement record (`access.granted`) | Before access is used (internal: 3 BD provisioning SLA) |
  | Annual review window or role change occurs (`access.review_due`) | Current entitlements (`access.entitlements[]`), role definition (`user.role`), last review date (`access.last_reviewed_at`) | Certification or revocation per entitlement (`access.review_completed`) | Annually; 5 BD after role change; same day for termination (enforced by `access.review_timer`) |
  | Change proposed to a financial system or control configuration (`change.requested`) | Change description (`change.description`), approver distinct from implementer (`change.approver_id`), test evidence (`change.test_evidence`), rollback plan (`change.rollback_plan`) | Approved, implemented, and verified change record (`change.completed`) | Approval before implementation; emergency post-approval within 2 BD (enforced by `change.emergency_approval_timer`) |

- **ALERTS/METRICS:** Target zero entitlements past their review due date; alert on any same-day-termination revocation that misses the day boundary; count of emergency changes per quarter with percentage receiving post-approval inside 2 business days (target 100%); alert on any control-configuration change lacking a linked change record.

## IC-06 — Exception and Override Management {#ic-06-exception-and-override-management}

- **WHY (Reg cite):** Overrides are the highest-risk pathway around the controls that [12 CFR §741.3](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/section-741.3) requires as sound practice; capturing and reviewing them is what allows the Supervisory Committee to satisfy its duty under [12 U.S.C. §1761b](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1761b) that controls are effectively maintained rather than routinely bypassed.
- **SYSTEM BEHAVIOR:** Every control override or policy exception is captured at the moment it is invoked, with the overriding user, rationale, affected control, and approver recorded before or at execution — silent overrides are technically impossible because the override path is the only path around a blocking control. Exceptions above the invoking approver's limit in the [IC-03](#ic-03-authorization-and-approval-limits) authority matrix route automatically to a senior approver. A standing exception (one expected to recur, such as a compensating-control arrangement) must be converted into a registered exception with an expiry date no more than 12 months out; expired standing exceptions revert to blocking behavior automatically. Compliance produces override analytics — frequency, concentration by user and control, and trend — for management monthly and for Internal Audit and the Supervisory Committee quarterly via [IC-07](#ic-07-monitoring-and-self-assessment). Override records are append-only and write-restricted to the control engine itself; no role may edit or delete an override record.
- **EVENTS:**

  | When | What's needed | Produced (and logged) | Within |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | User invokes an override on a blocking control (`control.override_invoked`) | Affected control ID (`control.id`), rationale (`override.rationale`), approver identity (`override.approver_id`) | Approved or denied override record (`override.recorded`) | Approver decision before or at execution |
  | Override exceeds invoking approver's authority (`override.escalation_required`) | Override record (`override.recorded`), authority matrix entry (`authority.matrix_entry`), senior approver availability (`override.senior_approver_id`) | Senior approval or denial (`override.senior_decision_recorded`) | Same business day (enforced by `override.escalation_timer`) |
  | Standing exception registered or expiring (`exception.registered` / `exception.expiring`) | Exception scope (`exception.scope`), expiry date (`exception.expires_at`), risk acceptance (`exception.risk_acceptance`) | Registered exception with expiry, or automatic reversion to blocking (`exception.reverted`) | Expiry no more than 12 months out; reversion automatic at expiry (enforced by `exception.expiry_timer`) |
  | Monthly analytics cycle (`override.analytics_due`) | All override records for period (`override.recorded[]`), control register (`control.register`) | Override analytics pack for management (`override.analytics_published`) | 10 BD after month-end |

- **ALERTS/METRICS:** Alert when any user invokes more than 5 overrides in 30 days or any single control is overridden more than 10 times in a quarter; target zero overrides lacking an approver at execution; track standing exceptions approaching expiry (alert at 30 days); publish override-rate trend per control to the monitoring runbook.

## IC-07 — Monitoring and Self-Assessment {#ic-07-monitoring-and-self-assessment}

- **WHY (Reg cite):** [12 CFR Part 715](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/part-715) (see [§715.5](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/part-715/section-715.5)) requires an annual audit grounded in a functioning internal control structure, and [12 U.S.C. §1761b](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1761b) charges the Supervisory Committee with determining that controls are effectively maintained — which requires ongoing management testing and deficiency tracking between audits, consistent with the monitoring component of the COSO-aligned FFIEC framework.
- **SYSTEM BEHAVIOR:** Process owners perform quarterly control self-assessments (CSAs) against the control register, and Compliance performs risk-based management testing of key controls on a rotating schedule that covers every key control at least every 24 months. Every deficiency — from CSAs, management testing, Internal Audit findings, or examiner findings — is logged in a single deficiency register with a severity rating, accountable owner, and due date, and is tracked to verified remediation; closure requires evidence, not assertion. High-severity deficiencies are reported to the Supervisory Committee at its next meeting; the full monitoring pack (CSA results, testing results, deficiency aging, and override analytics from [IC-06](#ic-06-exception-and-override-management)) goes to the board and Supervisory Committee quarterly. Internal Audit's own audit planning and execution are out of scope and governed by the Audit Policy; this control covers management's first- and second-line monitoring only. The deficiency register is write-restricted to Compliance; owners update remediation status through workflow, not direct edits.
- **EVENTS:**

  | When | What's needed | Produced (and logged) | Within |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Quarterly CSA window opens (`csa.cycle_opened`) | Control register extract (`control.register`), prior CSA results (`csa.prior_results`), period override data (`override.recorded[]`) | Completed CSA per process with attested results (`csa.completed`) | 30 days from window open (enforced by `csa.cycle_timer`) |
  | Deficiency identified from any source (`deficiency.logged`) | Description and source (`deficiency.source`), severity (`deficiency.severity`), accountable owner (`deficiency.owner_id`) | Remediation plan with due date (`deficiency.plan_recorded`) | Plan within 15 BD (enforced by `deficiency.plan_timer`) |
  | Remediation evidence submitted (`deficiency.remediation_submitted`) | Evidence artifacts (`deficiency.evidence[]`), retest result (`deficiency.retest_result`) | Verified closure or reopened deficiency (`deficiency.closed` / `deficiency.reopened`) | 15 BD verification SLA |
  | Quarter-end reporting (`reporting.quarterly_due`) | CSA results (`csa.completed[]`), testing results (`testing.results[]`), deficiency aging (`deficiency.aging_report`), override analytics (`override.analytics_published`) | Board and Supervisory Committee monitoring pack (`reporting.quarterly_published`) | 45 days after quarter-end (enforced by `reporting.quarterly_timer`) |

- **ALERTS/METRICS:** CSA completion rate per cycle (target 100% within 30 days); deficiency aging with counts past due (target zero high-severity past due); percentage of deficiencies closed with verified evidence versus assertion (target 100% verified); time-to-remediate distribution by severity, reported quarterly.

## IC-08 — Audit Trail and Recordkeeping {#ic-08-audit-trail-and-recordkeeping}

- **WHY (Reg cite):** [12 CFR Part 748](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/part-748) requires records supporting suspicious-activity and security programs, [12 CFR Part 749](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/part-749) requires preservation of vital records, and [12 CFR §741.3](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/section-741.3) requires books and records sufficient for NCUA examination — collectively requiring complete, tamper-evident audit trails of transactions and control events.
- **SYSTEM BEHAVIOR:** Every transaction and every control event in this policy — approvals, blocks, overrides, reconciliation sign-offs, access changes, configuration changes — is written to an append-only audit log keyed to the platform event stream (`event.type`, `event.resource_id`, `event.data`, `event.previous_data`), with each bookkeeping entry carrying a verifiable running balance (`bookkeeping_entry.running_balance`) so that any gap or alteration is detectable. Audit records cannot be edited or deleted by any user role, including administrators; corrections are made by compensating entries that reference the original. Control documentation — the control register, conflict and authority matrices (versioned), reconciliation packs, CSA and testing results, override records, and deficiency files — is retained for the longer of the platform retention class (5–7 years per entity, per vocabulary) or the period required by the Record Retention Policy, whose general schedules govern outside this control's scope. Retention holds for litigation or examination override normal disposal. Audit-log read access is granted to Compliance, Internal Audit, the Supervisory Committee, and examiners; no role holds write access to historical records.
- **EVENTS:**

  | When | What's needed | Produced (and logged) | Within |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Any transaction or control event occurs (`event.created`) | Event type and resource (`event.type`, `event.resource_id`), state snapshot (`event.data`), prior state for updates (`event.previous_data`) | Immutable audit record in the event store (`audit.record_written`) | Real time (write-ahead of business effect) |
  | Periodic integrity verification runs (`audit.integrity_check_due`) | Event sequence (`event.id[]`), running balances (`bookkeeping_entry.running_balance`), reconciliation output (`recon.daily_completed`) | Integrity attestation or gap alert (`audit.integrity_verified` / `audit.gap_detected`) | Daily (enforced by `audit.integrity_timer`) |
  | Record reaches end of retention class (`record.retention_expired`) | Retention class (`record.retention_class`), active hold status (`record.hold_status`) | Disposal or hold continuation, both logged (`record.disposed` / `record.hold_applied`) | Per retention class, 5–7 years minimum (enforced by `record.retention_timer`) |

- **ALERTS/METRICS:** Target zero integrity-check gaps (`audit.gap_detected` count = 0); alert immediately on any attempted write or delete against historical audit records; daily completeness ratio of control events with matching audit records (target 100%); count of disposals executed while a hold was active (target zero).

## Assumptions & Gaps {#assumptions}

- **Engineering vocabulary is provisional.** The Cassandra Banking Core vocabulary registers no events (`events` array is empty) and no timers or state machines. All `event.code`, field, and timer references in the EVENTS tables that are not among the 14 registered entities' fields — including `control.*`, `sod.*`, `authority.*`, `recon.*`, `access.*`, `change.*`, `override.*`, `exception.*`, `csa.*`, `deficiency.*`, `reporting.*`, `audit.*`, `record.*`, `gl.trial_balance`, `ledger.subsidiary_totals[]`, `process.*`, `user.role`, `transaction.*`, and `testing.results[]` — use the target naming scheme and must be registered by engineering before the next review. Registered fields cited from vocabulary (`bookkeeping_entry.running_balance`, `account.balances.balance`, `account.blnk_ledger_id`, `bookkeeping.account_code_5300`, `event.type`, `event.resource_id`, `event.data`, `event.previous_data`) are confirmed.
- **Reconciliation deadlines are internal judgments.** PATRICK_NOTES require daily reconciliation for cash and high-volume accounts and monthly for others but do not set completion times; the next-business-day 12:00 daily deadline, 10-business-day monthly close, and 30/90-day aged-item ladder are proposed standards needing CCO and Finance confirmation.
- **Dual-control thresholds are unspecified.** The transaction types and dollar bands triggering dual control in [IC-03](#ic-03-authorization-and-approval-limits) are placeholders pending the first approved authority matrix.
- **Override and CSA cadence assumptions.** Monthly override analytics, quarterly CSA cycles, the 24-month key-control testing rotation, and the 45-day quarterly reporting deadline are inferred minimum-viable cadences; PATRICK_NOTES require the activities but not the frequencies.
- **Single-approver governance.** Only the CCO is listed as approver. Board approval of the framework is asserted in [IC-01](#ic-01-control-environment-and-governance) per Federal Credit Union Act expectations, but the board's formal place in this policy's approval chain needs confirmation.
- **Severity taxonomy not yet defined.** The high/other severity split driving Supervisory Committee reporting in [IC-07](#ic-07-monitoring-and-self-assessment) assumes a deficiency severity scale that does not yet exist; it should align with the Enterprise Risk Management Policy taxonomy once confirmed.
- **Retention class mapping pending.** The 5–7 year retention floors come from the platform vocabulary's per-entity retention tags; the authoritative mapping of control records to retention schedules belongs to the Record Retention Policy and has not been reconciled against it.
