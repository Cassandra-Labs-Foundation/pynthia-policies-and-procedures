**Loan Collection and Charge-Offs**

Bank management has the responsibility for recognizing, reporting, monitoring and controlling delinquencies at proper levels and for the prompt and diligent follow-up of deteriorating credits.  Regular procedures should be established for routine follow-up and collection of delinquent installment and mortgage loans.  Immediate attention should be given to all loans when any difficulty becomes apparent in regard to interest payments or agreed-upon principal reductions. 

As potential difficulties arise, it is expected that management will take timely and appropriate action to assist the borrower in correcting these problems, if possible, and to protect the Bank from loss.  Particular attention obviously should be paid to loans subject to classification in recent regulatory agency examinations.  The Board should be kept apprised of the status of all problem loans on a regular and ongoing basis.

**Charge-Offs**

It shall be the policy of the Bank to charge off all, or portions of, those loans that are deemed by management to be uncollectible.

When, in the opinion of management, a portion or all of an outstanding loan has doubtful collectibility, that balance should be charged off after Board consultation and approval.  Normally, unsecured loans more than six months delinquent in the payment of principal or interest should be charged off in total.  Where secured, a charge-off normally should be made to reduce the balance to a level equal to the liquidation value of the collateral when payment of principal or interest is six months delinquent.

When a real estate loan is foreclosed upon and title to the property taken, the resulting O.R.E. should be booked at the “present realizable value” and the loss, if any, charged off to the loan reserve.

While it is recognized that a loan may appear uncollectible in whole, or in part, and therefore, appropriately charged to the Loan Loss Reserve, it is required that vigorous effort to collect such a loan continue unabated and unaffected by the fact that it is charged off, until such time as its ultimate uncollectibility is finally determined.

**Collection Practices**

Collection of any loan *secured by residential real property*, including home equity and home improvement loans, shall follow the policies and procedures outlined in the Residential Mortgage Loan Policies and Procedures adopted by the Board of \[Name of Institution\].

For loans *not secured by residential real property*:

* **10 days delinquent** — Bank shall send the borrower a past-due notice.

* **20 days delinquent** — Bank shall mail a follow-up notice reminding the borrower of the past-due obligation.

* **30 days delinquent** — The loan officer will make a personal contact and remind the borrower of the obligation and inform the borrower of the consequences of failure to pay.

* **60 days delinquent** — The loan officer will make personal contact. Notice of Right to Cure typically shall be mailed out at this time.

* **90 days delinquent** — After proper notice has been given and collateral protects the Bank from loss, repossess the collateral and begin liquidation.  If collateral does not protect the Bank from loss, other workout procedures may be necessary.  Determine loans which are not collectible with anticipated deficiencies charged against the loan reserve.  Pursue legal action and reduce to judgment.

There obviously will be times when it will be in the Bank’s interest to deviate from the above policy.  When this is the case, the loan officer should notify the \[Senior Loan Officer\] of the action and document the reason in the file.

Extension of payments on installment notes should be used only when sufficient justification can be given by the borrower.  The renewal of single pay term notes and/or consolidation of various notes normally is to be done only with full collection of interest from the borrower, with comments justifying such extensions and/or consolidations contained in the file memos.

**Guidelines for Retail Credit**

Evidence of the quality of consumer credit soundness is indicated best by the repayment performance demonstrated by the borrower.  Because retail credit generally is comprised of a large number of relatively small-balance loans, evaluating the quality of the retail credit portfolio on a loan-by-loan basis can be time consuming.  The Bank has therefore adopted the following guidelines that have been updated and expanded by the Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council (FFIEC).

Retail credit includes open-end and closed-end credit extended to individuals for household, family and other personal expenditures.  It includes consumer loans and credit cards.  For the purpose of this policy, retail credit also includes loans to individuals secured by their personal residence, including home equity and home improvement loans.

* Open-end credit will be charged off at 180 days delinquency from contractual due date;  
* Closed-end credit will be charged off at 120 days delinquency from contractual due date;  
* Open-end and closed-end retail loans past due 90 cumulative days from the contractual due date should be classified as substandard;  
* Accounts in bankruptcy should be charged off within 60 days of receipt of notification of filing from bankruptcy court, unless it can be clearly demonstrated and documented that repayment is likely;  
* Fraudulent loans should be charged off within 90 days of discovery;  
* 1-4 family residential real estate loans and home equity loans that are delinquent 90 days or more with loan-to-value of greater than \[60%\] should be classified as substandard;  
* When a residential or home equity loan is 120 days past due for closed-end credit and 180 days past due for open-end credit, a current assessment of value should be made and any outstanding loan balance in excess of the fair value of the property, less cost to sell, should be classified as loss;  
* Home equity loans where the Bank does not hold the senior mortgage that are delinquent 90 days or more should be classified substandard (even if the loan-to-value ratio is equal to, or less than, \[60%\]);  
* Loans of deceased persons should be charged off when the loss is determined.

In all cases that refer to a charge-off, the charge-off should be taken at the end of the month in which the period ends, regardless of whether it is 60, 90, 120 or 180 days.

If the delinquent loan is well secured and in the process of collection, such that collection or repayment will occur regardless of delinquency status, then the loan need not be classified. Such repayment, in spite of delinquency status, must be clearly documented in the file if the loan is not classified in accordance with these guidelines.

A well secured loan is collateralized by a perfected security interest in, or pledges of, real or personal property, including securities, with an estimated fair value, less cost to sell, sufficient to recover the recorded investment in the loan.  Loans for which claims are filed against solvent estates and loans supported by valid insurance claims could also be considered well secured. “In the process of collection” means that either a collection effort or legal action is proceeding and is reasonably expected to result in recovery of the loan balance or its restoration to a current status, generally within the next 90 days.
