**POLICY STATEMENT**

It is the policy of America’s Credit Union, a Federal Credit Union (ACU) to make our credit products available to all qualified applicants without discrimination on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, marital or familial status, age (provided the applicant has the capacity to contract), handicap, receipt of public assistance, or good faith exercise of rights under the Consumer Credit Protection Act (the prohibited basis).  ACU’s staff will not discourage the completion or submission of an application for credit by any applicant on any of the prohibited bases.  ACU’s staff will treat all consumers/members consistently and fairly and in compliance with fair lending laws.  ACU’s staff will be expected to offer assistance, encouragement and service in a fair, equitable and consistent manner.

**PURPOSE**

The purpose of this policy is to ensure ACU’s staff understands and fully complies with the provisions and protections provided to our consumer’s/member’s under the Fair Lending laws and regulations.  Fair Lending laws include the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (Regulation B), Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (Regulation C), the Fair Housing Act (FHA) and the NCUA’s Part 701.31 nondiscrimination requirements. 

**SCOPE**

The success of ACU’s Fair Lending Program is the responsibility of the entire organization.  ACU’s fair lending practices apply across all aspects of our operations, including marketing, loan origination, processing, underwriting, servicing, and collection activities.  ACU’s commitment to fair lending principles is demonstrated in terms of our structure and organization, employee accountability, monitoring and auditing programs, training and technology.  Our Chief Lending Officer is responsible for establishing practices designed to ensure that our operations reflect our strong commitment to fair lending and that all employees are aware of that commitment. 

**TYPES OF DISCRIMINATION**   

The courts have recognized three methods of proof of lending discrimination under the ECOA and the Fair Housing Act that credit union staff must be mindful of: 

**Overt evidence of  Discrimination** \- There is overt evidence of discrimination if the lender openly discriminates on a prohibited basis.  There also may be overt discrimination if a lender expresses, but does not act on, a discriminatory preference.

Overt discrimination may be one or more acts of discrimination by one or more loan officers, or it may be contained in a credit union policy that is overtly discriminatory.

**Comparative evidence of Disparate Treatment** \- 

Disparate treatment could occur if the credit union treated credit applicants differently based on one or more of the prohibited bases.  The difference in treatment does not have to be motivated by prejudice or a conscious intention to discriminate to be considered discriminatory.

Disparate treatment may involve unequal assistance being given to different applicants in the application process, or the presence or absence of discussion and investigation to clear up credit report problems, if this assistance to applicants varies on a prohibited basis.

**Disparate Impact –** 

If the credit union applies a policy or practice equally to credit applicants, but the policy or practice has a disproportionate adverse impact on applicants from a group protected against discrimination, the policy or practice is described as having a disparate impact.  Not every member of the group must be adversely affected for the practice to have a disparate impact.

A quantitative or statistical analysis is usually required to prove the existence of disparate impact.  If the credit union policy is based on “business necessity,” the credit union must be able to justify that a less discriminatory alternative policy does not exist.

**GATHERING, PROCESSING AND EVALUATING THE APPLICATION**

All prospective borrowers shall be encouraged to complete an application.  The Credit Union is required to obtain a written application for requests for a loan to purchase or refinance the purchase of a dwelling that is, or shall be, owner-occupied.  However, it is the policy of this credit union to require a written application to support all loan requests.  

No unnecessary information shall be gathered for the credit decision and credit union staff shall not ask prohibited questions. All credit decisions shall be made based on the creditworthiness of the applicant(s).  The credit union’s application forms shall state that income from alimony, child support, or separate maintenance payments need only be provided if the applicant is relying on that income for repayment of the credit requested.  

The age of the applicant may only be considered in determining the applicant’s ability to enter into a binding contract.  Credit may not be denied or terminated based on the applicant’s inability to obtain credit insurance due to their age. 

The credit union shall not require the signature of a spouse or person other than the credit applicant when the individual applicant qualifies for the credit requested.  When there is a joint applicant, the application shall include proper evidence that the applicants intended to apply for joint credit.

The credit union may request information about a spouse or former spouse of an applicant if: 

* the spouse shall be permitted to use the account;  
* the spouse shall be contractually liable on the account;  
* the applicant is relying on the spouse’s income, or the alimony, child support, or separate maintenance payments from a spouse or former spouse as a basis for repayment; or  
* the applicant is relying on property located in a community property state as a basis for repayment.

If an applicant applies for individual unsecured credit, credit union staff is prohibited from inquiring about the applicant's marital status, unless the applicant resides in a community property state or is relying on property located in such a state as a basis for repayment of the credit requested. 

If an application is for other than individual unsecured credit (joint and/or secured credit), credit union staff may inquire about the applicant's marital status, but may use only the terms married, unmarried, and separated. Credit union staff may explain that the category "unmarried" includes single, divorced, and widowed persons.

Credit union staff may not inquire about the sex of an applicant. An applicant may be requested to designate a title on an application form (such as Ms., Miss, Mr., or Mrs.) if the form discloses that the designation of a title is optional. An application form may otherwise use only terms that are neutral as to sex.

Credit union staff may not inquire about birth control practices, intentions concerning the bearing or rearing of children, or capability to bear children. Credit union staff may however inquire about the number and ages of an applicant's dependents or about dependent-related financial obligations or expenditures provided such information is requested without regard to sex, marital status, or any other prohibited basis.

Credit union staff may not inquire about the race, color, religion, or national origin of an applicant or any other person in connection with a credit transaction. However, credit union staff may inquire about an applicant's permanent residence and immigration status.

The Credit Union may require a qualified cosigner if the applicant does not qualify.  A signature may also be required on a security agreement for any joint owners of collateral that secures the loan.

**GOVERNMENT MONITORING INFORMATION (GMI)**

The credit union will gather certain information for government monitoring purposes in connection with credit applications primarily for the purchase or refinancing of a dwelling to be used as the applicant's principal residence where the extension of credit will be secured by the dwelling.  For purposes of GMI, "dwelling" means a residential structure that contains one to four units, whether or not that structure is attached to real property. The term includes, but is not limited to, an individual condominium or cooperative unit, and a mobile or other manufactured home. 

Credit union staff will request the following information as part of the application regarding the applicant(s): 

* ethnicity, using the categories Hispanic or Latino, and Not Hispanic or Latino; and race, using the categories American Indian or Alaskan Native, Asian, Black or African American; Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander, and White;   
* sex;   
* marital status, using the categories married, unmarried, and separated; and   
* age. 

The credit union staff will also inform the applicant(s) that the information regarding race or national origin, sex, marital status, and age is being requested by the Federal government for the purpose of monitoring compliance with Federal statutes that prohibits the credit union from discriminating against applicants on those bases.

**REAL ESTATE APPRAISALS**  

The credit union shall make available, to any requesting member/applicant, a copy of the appraisal used in connection with that member's real estate-related loan application in compliance with NCUA’s nondiscrimination regulation. The appraisal will be available for 25 months after the applicant has received a notice of action taken by the credit union on the real estate-related loan application.

Effective January 18, 2014 the credit union will be subject to Regulation B, Section 1002.14 for rules on providing appraisals and other evaluations that cover all first liens on dwellings, including closed-end mortgage loans and open-end loans as follows:

*    the credit union will provide a disclosure to applicants that they have the right to receive copies of appraisals and written valuations, and  
*    the credit union will automatically send a free copy of home appraisals and other written valuations promptly after they are completed, regardless of whether credit is extended, denied, incomplete, or withdrawn. This applies to all written valuations (not just appraisals) that are developed in connection with an application for a first lien on dwellings.

**NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS**  

The credit union will notify applicants of each loan decision reached by the credit union for any application received, and will strictly comply with the notification and adverse action notices required by the Equal Credit Opportunity Act and Regulation B.  The credit union will also comply with Section 615(a) of the Fair Credit Reporting Act when adverse action is taken against an applicant based in whole or in part on information in a consumer report.  In addition, if the credit score is used in taking adverse action the credit union will also include the required information relating to that score on the adverse action notice.       

The credit union will notify an applicant of action taken within:

* 30 days after receiving a completed application concerning the creditor's approval of, counteroffer to, or adverse action on the application;  
* 30 days after taking adverse action on an incomplete application,   
* 30 days after taking adverse action on an existing account; or  
* 90 days after notifying the applicant of a counteroffer if the applicant does not expressly accept or use the credit offered; or the credit union may choose to send an adverse action letter at the same time of the counteroffer within 30 days of receiving a completed application.

For incomplete applications, the credit union may send either a notice of incompleteness or an adverse-action notice. If a notice of incompleteness is sent, it will be specific about what information is missing and provide a reasonable time for the applicant to provide it. The notice will state that if the missing information is not provided within a designated time, no further consideration will be given to the application.

**RECORD RETENTION REQUIREMENTS**  

The credit union will comply with all Regulation B retention requirements and retain the original or a copy of the following documents for 25 months after the notice of action is taken:

* the application and the information obtained for purposes of monitoring compliance with anti-discrimination statutes;  
* any written information used in evaluating the request; and  
* a copy of the notification provided to an applicant and a statement of specific reasons for any adverse action.

All records related to prescreened solicitations will also be maintained for 25 months.  

**FAIR HOUSING ACT**

The Fair Housing Act provides for fair housing throughout the United States.  Credit union staff will not deny a loan or other financial assistance for the purpose of purchasing, constructing, improving, repairing, or maintaining a dwelling, nor may it discourage an application for such a loan, on the basis of the race, color, religion, handicap, familial status (having children under the age of 18), national origin, or sex of: 

* The loan applicant or joint applicant   
* Any person associated with the loan applicant or joint applicant   
* The present or prospective owners, lessees, tenants, or occupants of other dwellings in the vicinity of the dwelling 

Credit Union staff will ensure all credit-related decisions will be based on neutral credit factors such as the applicant’s income, credit history, length and stability of employment, ratio of income to current and proposed financial obligations, and ratio of the proposed principal balance of the loan to the value of the proposed property security. 

Unless a valid and well-documented exception exists, the credit union will not consider any of the following factors in connection with a real estate-related loan:

* the age or location of the dwelling;  
* the age or location of dwellings in the neighborhood of the dwelling;  
* the income level of residents in the neighborhood of the dwelling;  
* the zip code of the applicant’s current residence; or  
* previous home ownership.

In addition, credit union staff will not use the services of an appraiser who is known to improperly consider any of the prohibited bases.

**ADVERTISING AND LOBBY DISCLOSURES**

The credit union shall not engage in advertising in a manner that would discourage, on a prohibited basis, the making or pursuing of an application for credit.  As applicable, advertising shall include the Equal Housing Lender logotype or the term “Equal Housing Lender”.  The disclosure will be made in a manner appropriate to the advertising medium and format used that ensures it is clear and conspicuous.    
   
Each of the Credit Union’s locations shall maintain an Equal Housing Lender poster in the public lobby of the credit union and in the public area of each office where real estate related loans are made.  The notice must be clearly visible to the general public.

**HOME MORTGAGE DISCLOSURE ACT (HMDA)** 

The credit union will comply with the Home Mortgage Disclosure Act and properly compile and disclose data about home purchase loans, home improvement loans, and refinancings that we originate or purchase, or for which we receive applications.  Reference ACU’s HMDA Policy.

**THIRD PARTIES**

Applicable agreements and contracts with third party service providers will contain language which indicates the third party acknowledges and agrees to abide by fair lending laws. 

**PERIODIC REVIEWS**

Third party services will be contracted to conduct periodic risk based reviews in all areas of fair lending.  The reviewers will utilize NCUA’s Fair Lending Guide to establish the review areas and to make use of the various checklist tools available.  The Executive Management and the Board of Directors will be provided with a written report for all periodic fair lending reviews conducted.  

**TRAINING**

The Training Solutions Manager will provide and/or facilitate the instruction of periodic fair lending training to all applicable staff based on job responsibilities. The ongoing training program will provide staff with the basic principles and core requirements of the various fair lending laws and regulations that impact their job functions.
