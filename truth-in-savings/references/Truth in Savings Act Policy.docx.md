**Truth in Savings Act Policy**

It is the policy of \[name of financial institution\] to comply with the requirements of the Truth in Savings Act (TISA) and its implementing regulations in Regulation DD. TISA requires depository institutions to disclose the terms of deposit accounts to consumers, imposes requirements for advertising of deposit accounts, and stipulates disclosure requirements on certain overdraft programs. 

Thus, \[name of financial institution\] management is charged with implementing procedures as necessary to see that \[name of financial institution\] and its employees comply with the requirements of Regulation DD including:

* The general disclosure requirement that depository institutions must provide to consumers written disclosures about the terms of deposit accounts in a form they can keep. The disclosures must reflect the terms of the deposit contract and must be understandable and noticeable. Requirements also provide rules for quoting rates.

* Required account disclosures containing specified information that must be provided to a consumer before an account is opened or a service is provided. If the consumer is not present at the institution when the account is opened, the institution must mail or deliver the disclosure(s) within ten business days. 

* Subsequent disclosure requirements that a consumer must receive advance notice from the depository institution if any term is changed, if the change may reduce the annual percentage yield or adversely affect the consumer. The notice must include the effective date and must be mailed or delivered at least thirty calendar days before the effective date of the change.   
    
* Requirements that depository institutions send notices shortly before maturity of most automatically renewable (rollover) accounts such as certificates of deposit. Similar notices are also required for long-term accounts that do not automatically renew.

* Periodic statement disclosures that detail the types of information that a depository institution must include in a periodic statement if the institution chooses to provide statements.

* Payment of interest requirements that depository institutions are to pay interest on the full principal balance in an account each day. The requirements stipulate the methods institutions may use to calculate account balances: the daily balance and average daily balance methods.

* Advertising requirements, which include that an advertisement must not be inaccurate or misleading and must not misrepresent a depository institution's deposit contract. The requirements discuss when institutions may advertise an account as "free" and require an institution to disclose additional terms about a deposit account if it advertises rates or bonuses paid on the account.

* Record retention, which requires institutions to retain records of their compliance with the regulation for two years.

* Additional disclosure requirements for overdraft services including disclosure of total fees on periodic statements, advertising disclosures for overdraft services, and disclosure of account balances through automated systems.
