# Director Fiduciary Duties — Drafting Notes

## Scope (Patrick)
*"Mostly about Conflict of Interest Policies and the duties they owe to the company itself."*

Broader than just conflict of interest — covers the full **fiduciary duties** owed by directors / officers / trustees:
- **Duty of Loyalty** — act in best interests of the company, not personal interests. Conflict of interest disclosure is the operational implementation of this.
- **Duty of Care** — informed decision-making, board attendance, review of materials before voting.
- **Duty of Good Faith** — no fraud, no intentional misconduct, no knowing violations of law.
- **Duty of Confidentiality** — protect non-public information.
- **Duty to Disclose** — proactive disclosure of conflicts as they arise (continuing obligation).

Conflict of Interest is one operational sub-section; this policy is the umbrella.

## DFPI scope
- California state law: **California Corporations Code §§ 309** (director duty of care), **§ 310** (interested-director transactions), **§ 5230 et seq.** for nonprofit boards. For state-chartered financial institutions, **California Financial Code** also imposes duties.
- For federally insured institutions (if applicable): **12 USC § 1828(k)** (golden parachute / indemnification restrictions), **Part 359** (FDIC golden parachute rule).
- DFPI directly examines director independence and conflict management for state-chartered banks/credit unions.

## Canonical drafting work needed (replace stub)
Suggested structure:
1. **Purpose & Scope** — applies to directors, officers, employees, contractors with decision authority.
2. **The Fiduciary Duties** — define each (loyalty, care, good faith, confidentiality, disclosure).
3. **Conflict of Interest Identification** — what counts (financial, familial, business relationships, gifts).
4. **Disclosure Process** — annual questionnaire + continuing obligation + ad-hoc disclosures.
5. **Management of Conflicts** — recusal, abstention from vote, independent review, board determination.
6. **Insider Transactions** — Reg O (loans to insiders), board-approval thresholds, FDIC/DFPI notification.
7. **Gifts & Entertainment** — de minimis threshold (commonly $100/$250), prohibition on cash, vendor relationships.
8. **Outside Business Activities** — pre-approval requirements.
9. **Recordkeeping** — retain disclosures (cross-reference `record-retention/` — typically permanent for directors).
10. **Training & Acknowledgment** — annual training, annual signed acknowledgment.
11. **Enforcement** — escalation, removal, board sanctions.
12. **Exhibits:**
    - Exhibit A: Annual disclosure questionnaire
    - Exhibit B: Ad-hoc disclosure form
    - Exhibit C: De minimis gift threshold schedule
