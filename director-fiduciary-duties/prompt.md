## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy sets out the fiduciary duties owed to Pynthia Credit Union by its directors, officers, and other persons with the power to direct management or policy, and the related conflict-of-interest, insider-transaction, and recordkeeping requirements they must observe. It applies to all directors, executive officers, principal shareholders, and any employee or contractor with decision-making authority, and covers the full set of fiduciary duties — loyalty, care, good faith, confidentiality, and the continuing obligation to disclose — with conflict-of-interest management as the operational implementation of the duty of loyalty.

## 2. Key Regulatory Authorities

- **Federal Reserve Act §§ 22(g) and (h) / Regulation O** — 12 U.S.C. §§375a, 375b; 12 CFR Part 215; restrictions, prior-Board-approval thresholds, and aggregate limits on extensions of credit to insiders (directors, executive officers, principal shareholders, and their related interests).
- **Federal Reserve Act §§ 23A and 23B / Regulation W** — 12 U.S.C. §§371c, 371c-1; 12 CFR Part 223; quantitative limits, collateral, and market-terms requirements on covered transactions with affiliates.
- **Federal Bank Bribery Law** — 18 U.S.C. §215; Guidelines for Compliance With the Federal Bank Bribery Law, 52 Fed. Reg. 43941 (1987); prohibition on soliciting or receiving anything of value in connection with credit union business, with limited gift/entertainment exceptions.
- **NCUA Director Duties (Federal Credit Unions)** — 12 CFR §701.4; general authorities and duties of federal credit union directors: (1) duty of care and loyalty — carry out duties in good faith, in the best interests of the membership as a whole, with the care an ordinarily prudent person in a like position would use under similar circumstances, including reasonable inquiry; (2) duty of impartiality — administer affairs fairly and without discrimination in favor of or against any particular member; (3) financial literacy requirement — within 6 months of election or appointment, have at least a working familiarity with basic finance and accounting, including ability to read the balance sheet and income statement and ask substantive questions of management and auditors. Note: §701.4 applies to federal charters; Legal should confirm whether Pynthia is a federal or state charter and substitute or supplement citations accordingly.
- **Conflicts of Interest and Corporate Opportunity** — 12 CFR §§563.200 and 563.201; duty to disclose interests, abstain from related decisions, and not usurp corporate opportunities.
- **RESPA anti-tying / Home Owners' Loan Act tying** — 12 U.S.C. §§2608, 1464(q); prohibition on prohibited tie-in arrangements.
- **California Corporations Code §§ 309 and 310** — director duty of care and the standard for interested-director transactions; **California Financial Code** duties for state-chartered institutions examined by DFPI.

## 3. What This Policy Must Cover

The core risk is self-dealing and undisclosed conflicts that advance personal interests at the credit union's expense, plus regulatory limits on insider credit and affiliate transactions. The policy is the umbrella over the duties; conflict-of-interest disclosure is its primary operational mechanism.

The policy must establish the following controls:

**(a) The Fiduciary Duties.** Define and require adherence to the duties of loyalty (act in the credit union's best interest, not personal interest), care (informed decision-making, attendance, review of materials before voting), good faith (no fraud, intentional misconduct, or knowing violations of law), confidentiality (protect non-public information), and disclosure (proactive, continuing disclosure of conflicts). Per 12 CFR §701.4, also require: impartiality (fair administration without discrimination in favor of or against any particular member) and financial literacy (each director must attain, within 6 months of election or appointment, at least a working familiarity with basic finance and accounting, including the ability to read the credit union's balance sheet and income statement and to ask substantive questions of management and the auditors; track attainment and flag overdue directors).

**(b) Conflict Identification & General Duties.** Require that covered persons not advance their own or related parties' interests at the credit union's expense, disclose to the Board all material information on any matter in which they have an interest, and not participate in the Board's discussion or vote on that matter; avoid even the appearance of a conflict.

**(c) Annual & Continuing Disclosure.** Require an annual disclosure questionnaire from each director and officer (Exhibit A), an ad-hoc disclosure form when a conflict arises mid-year (Exhibit B, continuing obligation), and an annual record of insiders and their related interests reviewed for completeness.

**(d) Management of Conflicts.** Provide for recusal, abstention from voting, independent review, and Board determination on conflicted matters; interested parties may not influence the Board's deliberations.

**(e) Insider Transactions (Reg O / 12 CFR §701.21(d)).** Require that extensions of credit to insiders be on substantially the same terms and underwriting as comparable non-insider transactions; require prior Board approval (excluding the interested director) when aggregate credit to an insider and related interests exceeds the greater of $25,000 or 5% of unimpaired capital and surplus, or $500,000 (Reg O thresholds, adopted as internal standards per Patrick's direction); note that 12 CFR §701.21(d) — the NCUA-specific provision for federal credit unions — sets the Board-approval trigger at $20,000 plus pledged shares, which Legal should confirm as the operative threshold if Pynthia is a federal charter; observe the single-borrower lending limit (15%, plus 10% for readily marketable collateral) and the aggregate insider limit; report extensions promptly to the Board.

**(f) Transactions With Affiliates (Reg W).** Limit covered transactions to 10% of unimpaired capital and surplus per affiliate and 20% in the aggregate, require collateral and market terms, prohibit purchase of low-quality assets, and maintain an affiliate list updated at least annually.

**(g) Bank Bribery, Gifts & Kickbacks.** Prohibit soliciting or receiving anything of value in connection with credit union business; permit only de minimis gifts/entertainment within defined thresholds (Exhibit C); require disclosure of offers beyond what is authorized, with records reported to the Board.

**(h) Corporate Opportunity & Tie-Ins.** Prohibit usurping corporate opportunities belonging to the credit union and prohibited tie-in arrangements; document Board rejection of opportunities as a matter of sound business judgment.

**(i) Recordkeeping & Reporting.** Retain disclosures, insider/related-interest records, affiliate transaction records, and gift disclosures, and provide public disclosure of insider credit on written request as required.

**(j) Training, Acknowledgment & Enforcement.** Distribute the policy to all directors and officers, require an annual signed acknowledgment and annual training, and define escalation, removal, and Board sanctions for willful violations (return of benefits, resignation, or dismissal).

Governance of these controls is centralized with the Chief Compliance Officer, with the Board of Directors, Legal/General Counsel, and the Supervisory Committee as required participants.
