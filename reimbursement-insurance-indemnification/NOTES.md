## Likely scope
1. **Reimbursement of business expenses** — travel, lodging, meals, education/training, board materials. Pre-approval thresholds, receipt requirements, reasonable-and-customary standards.
2. **Insurance program** — what coverages are maintained, in what amounts, for whom (directors, officers, employees, committee members).
3. **Indemnification** —
   - Mandatory indemnification (when D/O is successful on merits).
   - Permissive indemnification (when settlement or judgment against D/O, subject to standards).
   - Advancement of expenses (during defense, before final resolution).
   - Exclusions (bad faith, knowing violation of law, breach of duty of loyalty, etc.).
4. **Process** — who decides indemnification claims (board minus interested directors / independent counsel opinion / shareholders).
5. **Procedures for claims** — notification to carrier, cooperation, document preservation, communications.

## DFPI / regulatory scope
- **California Corporations Code §§ 204(a)(10), 317** — corporate indemnification framework.
- **FDIC Part 359** (Golden Parachute and Indemnification Payments) — restricts indemnification payments by troubled institutions; the policy must carve out compliance with §359 even if not currently in troubled condition.
- **12 USC § 1828(k)** — federal restrictions on indemnification of institution-affiliated parties.
- **DFPI bond requirements** for state-chartered institutions (fidelity bond minimums).
