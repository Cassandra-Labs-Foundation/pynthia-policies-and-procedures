**Interest Rate Risk and Asset Liability Management**  
*Policy and Procedures*

**Purpose**

The purpose of our IRR and asset/liability policy and procedures is to establish a prudent and comprehensive mechanism to **identify, measure, monitor and control** interest rate risk.

As a part of this plan we are identifying four primary elements to effective interest rate risk management:

* A formalized policy adopted by the Board of Directors  
* Measurement of the level of risk   
* Limits upon the amount of risk which the bank is willing to accept   
* Regular reporting to Management and the Board to monitor the current and ongoing level of risk 

**General Goals and Objectives**

The primary objective of this policy is to provide guidelines for bank management to exercise flexibility in establishing interest rate risk positions.  In order to achieve this objective, the bank will endeavor to ensure both management and board awareness of the bank’s interest rate exposure, enable effective measurement and management of interest rate risk, select strategies that optimize the ability of the bank to meet its long-range financial objectives while maintaining interest rate risk within the policy limits established by the Board of Directors.

Of the items addressed in this plan are, the role of the Asset/Liability Committee (ALCO), Pricing Committee actions, Profit Planning and Liquidity guidelines.  The end result of this plan should be the maintenance of relatively consistent earnings in an environment of fluctuating interest rates.

**Board and Senior Management Oversight**

Effective board and senior management oversight of the bank’s interest rate risk activities is the cornerstone of a sound risk management process.  The board and senior management are responsible for understanding the nature and level of interest rate risk being taken by the bank and how these strategies fit in the normal operation of the bank.  They are also responsible for ensuring that the formality and sophistication of the risk management process is appropriate for the size of the bank and the overall level of risk being undertaken.  Effective risk management requires an informed board, capable management and appropriate staffing.

**Board of Directors**

For its part, the Bank’s Board of Directors have two broad responsibilities;

* **To establish and guide the bank tolerance for interest rate risk**, including approving relevant risk limits and other key policies, identifying lines of authority and responsibility for managing risk, and ensuring adequate resources are devoted to interest rate risk management.

* **To monitor the bank’s overall interest rate risk profile** and ensure that the level of interest rate risk is maintained at prudent levels.

**Senior Management**

Senior Management is responsible for ensuring that interest rate risk is managed on both a long range and day-to-day- basis.  In managing the bank’s activities, the Senior Management should:

* **Develop and implement policies and procedures** that translate the board’s goals, objectives and risk limits  into operating practices that are well understood by the bank’s personnel and that are consistent with the board’s intent.

* **Ensure adherence to the lines of authority and responsibility** that the board has approved for measuring, managing, monitoring and reporting interest rate risk exposures.

* **Oversee the implementation and maintenance** of management information and other systems that identify, measure, monitor and control the bank’s interest rate risk.

* **Establish internal controls** over the interest rate risk management process

**Asset/Liability Committee**

**Purpose**

The purpose of the ALCO is to establish, communicate, coordinate and control the management of the banks assets and liabilities.  The committee will establish and monitor the volume and mix of assets and funding sources.  The objective is to manage assets and funding sources to produce results what are consistent with the bank’s liquidity, capital adequacy, growth, risk, sensitivity and profitability goals.

**Committee Members**

The C.E.O., President, and Vice President shall have responsibility for, and perform the duties of, the Asset Liability Committee.  

**Responsibility and Authority**

Senior management of the bank will make available to the ALCO Committee any information necessary to support the committee’s activities.  At each meeting the Committee will review current information and trends and recommend appropriate changes based upon a review of information including, but not limited to, the following:

* Local, regional and national business and economic trends

* Forecast for changes in interest rates

* Anticipated changes in the volume and mix of the loan and investment portfolios

* Anticipated changes in the volume and mix of the sources of funds (Fed Funds, Core deposits, Certificates of deposit, savings, Brokered CD’s, etc..)

**Identification and Measurement of Interest Rate Risk**

The goal of asset/liability management is to maximize and stabilize the net interest margin while maintaining credit quality, reasonable interest rate risk, adequate capital as well as liquidity.  It is among the duties of the ALCO Committee to monitor the ratios of rate-sensitive assets (RSA) and rate-sensitive liabilities (RSL) to manage interest rate risk and keep it within acceptable levels.

Rate Sensitive is defined as anything maturing or re-pricing within the next 12 months.  Twelve months is considered an appropriate time frame for numerous reasons.  Forecasting is required in order to ascertain the volume and mix of RSA and RSL for the twelve month period.  Forecasting involved making informed assumptions about multiple variables in the future such as: interest rates, loan demand, deposit mix, bank growth, regulatory changes, etc.. As most of these variables are outside of the control of bank management, forecasting beyond twelve months would degrade the accuracy and reliability of the forecast and therefore will not be done for purposes of risk management.

An analysis of twelve months provides us with the opportunity to recognize these trends and provides us with adequate time to adjust as appropriate.

Traditionally, a zero gap was thought to provide zero interest rate risk.  In other words, if RSA were equal to RSL, then the institution had a zero gap and conventional wisdom was that interest rate changes would not impact earnings.  Recent studies however have shown that most institutions with zero gaps still encounter earnings impacts as rates change.  This is due to the fact that asset yields and liability costs do not behave the same in a changing interest rate environment.

**Earnings at Risk**

(Bank Name) will utilize a Total Bank Analysis Report (TBA) which is provided quarterly by First St. Louis Securities, Inc. which utilizes Plansmith Corporation Software.  The TBA will provide the bank with a gap analysis.  The gap analysis also provides the basis for a more detailed analysis in its simulation modeling.  While gap results are a popular rate risk indicator, simulation methods provide a more accurate report of the impact of an interest rate change on income as each categories interest rate change is calculated as rates ramp up or down.  In addition, the repayment speeds and re-pricing speeds are changed in this process. 

Rate Shock is a method utilized for stress testing of the Net Interest Margin (NIM) over the next four quarters under several different rate change scenarios.  These levels span 50 basis point (bp) increments up and down from the current interest rates in effect.  In order to simulate activity, maturing balances are replaced with the new balances at the new rate level and re-pricing balances are adjusted to the new rate shock level. The interest is recalculated fore each level along with the new average yields.  NIM is then calculated and a margin risk profile is developed.  This information is provided to the board on a quarterly basis.

Gap Report

The TBA report also provides a quarterly gap report which measures the level of earning assets and paying liabilities within a particular time frame.  The time frames include floating, 1-3 months, 3-12 months, 1-3 years, 3-5 ears 5-15 years and greater than 15 years. Gap is the dollar difference between RSA and RSL for the time frames noted above.

If more RSA re-price than RSL then the gap is said to be positive or asset sensitive.  An asset sensitive institution will generally benefit through increased interest income as the loans re-price upwards in greater magnitude than the bank’s liabilities.  Similarly, in a decreasing interest rate environment the bank will be hindered by more RSA re-pricing at a lower level as rates decrease while not having the opportunity to re-price their RSL.

If the bank has more RSL than RSA re-pricing then the opposite is true and the institution has a negative gap.  Similarly, a liability sensitive institution will realize increased income when the opposite is true, falling interest rates as the liabilities re-price more frequently lowering interest expense while interest income remains as the higher rates until those assets are re-priced.  This information is provided to the board on a quarterly basis.

**Economic Value of Equity**

Economic Value of Equity (EVA) is a measure of long term interest rate risk.  EVE is the difference between the market values of the RSA and RSL and actually represents a discounted present value of the liquidation value of the bank.  In this analysis the report calculated the discounted present value of the various cash flows associated with each category on the balance sheet under 9 separate rate conditions.

The percentage change in the Market Value of Equity (MVE) is also referred to as the Duration of Equity and is a measure of the volatility of the total value and therefore risk.  Each year equals a 1% change in market value given a 100 bp change in interest rates.  With the addition of a time dimension a longer duration in the values represents greater risk.  
MVE calculations require good cash flow numbers as well as some knowledge of embedded options for reasonable accuracy.  Because the system is calculating cash flows, it is possible to estimate the maturity and prepayment at all rate levels in order to approximate durations.  The market values for the major categories at various rate change levels are calculated utilizing their durations.  Loan market values are computed using discounted cash flows and current market rates.  The fair value of securities is taken from the bond report and call report information.  The results of these calculations are monitored to ensure that the bank’s interest rate exposure will not lead to unsafe and an  
unsound condition.  This information is provided to the board on a quarterly basis.

**Rate Shock of Investment Securities**

A monthly bond accounting report (BAR) is calculated by the Portfolio Evaluation Company monthly.  The BAR provides a detailed analysis of all investment securities of the bank.  This extrapolates the price volatility of the securities in the investment portfolio to determine the approximate capital impact to the bank should the bank’s SAFS portfolio be liquidated.  This analysis involves the determination of the change in the unrealized gain/loss on the securities portfolio given an instantaneous shift of the yield curve of \+/- 100, 200, 300, and 400 bp.  The results of these calculations and the make up of the portfolio are monitored to insure that the bank’s interest rate exposure will not lead to and unsafe and unsound condition.  This information is provided to the board on a monthly basis.

***Earnings at Risk***

For purposes of this policy, EAR will be measured by the changes in annual or annualized Net Interest Income (NIM).  As a part of the Asset/Liability management process, the ALCO will monitor through the TBA report quarterly, the change in Net Interest income given various changes in interest rates (plus/minus 100,200,300,400bp).  Should the indicated change in interest rates impact the bank’s NIM by greater than 15% the ALCO will take action(s) to reduce this exposure level to the guidance level or note in it’s meeting that the level is outside of the guidance level but acceptable, a reasonable projection for the period of time and actions which will be undertaken to return it to an acceptable level and this information shall be discussed in the next regular board meeting and noted in the minutes.

***Economic Value of Equity***

For purposes of this policy, the bank has a target variance of EVA less than or equal to 15%.  The bank’s current guidance is to maintain an interest rate position such that various rate shocks (plus/minus 100, 200, 300, 400bp), whichever direction is more adverse,will not cause the bank’s economic value (as shown by the Market Value of Equity \[MVE\] in the TBA report to decline more than 15%.

***Gap Ratio***

When the Gap Ratio according to the Gap Report reveals a mismatch between RSA and RSL within the 12 month horizon of greater than .70 (liability sensitive) to 1.30 (asset sensitive) then the ALCO will take action(s) to reduce the sensitivity to the guidance levels   or note in it’s meeting that the level is outside of the guidance level but acceptable, a reasonable projection for the  period of time and actions which will be undertaken to return it to an acceptable level and this information shall be discussed in the next regular board meeting and noted in the minutes.

In conjunction with the interest rate risk being quantified and acceptable levels of risk determined these risk limits address the risk to the bank’s earnings from mismatches between the re-pricing of assets and liabilities.

When the bank is outside of the acceptable range and asset sensitive, investments will be purchased with longer maturities and maturities of new loans and renewing loans may be extended, this will help correct this imbalance.  Conversely, when the bank becomes liability sensitive, time deposit and new investments’ maturities will be shortened.

**Liquidity**

Liquidity is a measure of the bank’s ability to raise cash at a reasonable cost in order to meet any and all obligations of its depositors at any given time.  As a bank we must be able to meet their cash withdrawal requirements and fund our loan commitments and available line of credit commitments and any other short term credit needs which may arise.

*Investment Portfolio*

The average maturity of the entire investment portfolio shall be five years or less and all bonds will be classified as available for sale.  

*Pledging*

The bank also will utilize Collateral Guaranty Bonds in lieu of pledging wherever practical, acceptable and economical at the discretion of the ALCO in order to maintain increased liquidity availability in the investment portfolio.  

*Borrowings*

Currently the bank has two sources of funds available to supplement our liquidity needs.

In addition, the bank has available borrowings through the Federal Home Loan Bank of Des Moines..

Management will utilize combinations of short term purchases of Fed Funds, available borrowings and securities sales to meet liquidity requirements

***Funding***

Core Deposits or deposits generated locally are the preferred funding source for the bank.  
However, due to competitive pressures from competing banks and alternative investments this may not always be the most economical source of funding.  The bank will utilize a combination of non-local deposits and brokered certificates of deposit to supplement the funding needs of the bank.. While recent evidence supports the assertion that that larger core deposit customers are at least as volatile a source of funding as non-local and brokered deposits, they continue to be viewed differently by our regulators.

Banks which have effective methodologies for identifying, measuring, monitoring and controlling these volatile non-core deposits can more efficiently raise funds, often at a discount to traditional core funding sources thus providing the bank with a more cost effective source of funds without cannibalizing existing accounts with “specials” and increasing their internal cost of funds on existing accounts.
