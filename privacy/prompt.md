## 1. Purpose and Scope

Pynthia Credit Union operates a risk-based privacy program governing how nonpublic personal information (NPPI) of members and consumers is collected, used, disclosed, safeguarded, corrected, and disposed of. The program complies with the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act and Regulation P, NCUA Part 748 information-security guidelines, FCRA/Reg V (including NCUA Part 717), the FACTA Disposal Rule, the Right to Financial Privacy Act, E-SIGN, and applicable U.S. state privacy laws where they touch non-GLBA data such as marketing telemetry. Scope includes all deposit, lending, payment, and online products, all directors, officers, employees, temporary staff, and vendors with access to NPPI, and information in any form.

## 2. Key Regulatory Authorities

- **GLBA / Regulation P (12 CFR Part 1016)** — Privacy notices, opt-out, sharing limits, and delivery/exceptions: initial (§1016.4), annual (§1016.5), revised (§1016.8), delivery (§1016.9), and exceptions (§1016.13–§1016.15).
- **NCUA Part 748 & Appendices A/B (12 CFR §748.0)** — Information-security program, incident-response program, and member-information safeguarding and vendor oversight.
- **FCRA / Reg V (12 CFR Part 1022)** — Accuracy of consumer information, address-discrepancy handling, and affiliate-marketing limits.
- **NCUA Part 717 (12 CFR Part 717)** — Credit-union implementation of FCRA, including Red Flags and address-discrepancy rules.
- **FACTA Disposal Rule (16 CFR Part 682)** — Reasonable measures for disposal of consumer report information.
- **Right to Financial Privacy Act (12 USC §3401 et seq.)** — Government access to financial records; member notice and process requirements.
- **E-SIGN (15 USC §7001)** — Electronic delivery of notices and capture of e-consent.
- **COPPA (16 CFR Part 312)** — Children's online privacy for users under 13.
- **State Privacy (U.S.)** — California/CPRA, Vermont, and Nevada requirements applied to non-GLBA data only (e.g., "Do Not Sell/Share," marketing limits).

## 3. What This Policy Must Cover

Privacy risk at Pynthia concentrates wherever NPPI is created, shared with third parties, accessed by members or staff, or disposed of, and at the boundary where non-GLBA marketing/telemetry data triggers stricter state-law obligations. The policy must operationalize the full GLBA notice lifecycle, enforce member choices, restrict and document permissible disclosures, and integrate privacy into incident response.

**Strictest-Standard Default.** Where two or more applicable laws govern the same data or processing activity, apply the stricter requirement to all members regardless of state of residence. For non-GLBA data this means CPRA-equivalent standards serve as the floor: collect only what is necessary for the stated purpose (data minimization), use data only for that purpose (purpose limitation), disclose retention periods and delete when no longer needed, honor the right to limit use of sensitive personal information, and extend access/deletion/correction rights to all members.

The policy must establish the following controls:

**(a) Privacy Notice Lifecycle.** Deliver initial notices at or before the relationship starts, annual notices every 12 months unless exempt, and revised notices prior to any material change in sharing, recording delivery channel, timestamp, and template id; block non-exception sharing until the notice is delivered (Reg P §1016.4, §1016.5, §1016.8, §1016.9).

**(b) Opt-Out Capture & Honoring.** Provide at least two opt-out channels, enforce suppression immediately, and propagate opt-outs to martech vendors within 30 days (program standard) (Reg P §1016.7).

**(c) Permissible Disclosures & Exceptions.** Tag each disclosure with a legal basis under the service-provider/joint-marketing (§1016.13), servicing/processing (§1016.14), and legal/protective (§1016.15) exceptions, and require a GLBA confidentiality clause in the vendor contract before sharing.

**(d) Customer Access & Authentication.** Verify identity before disclosing member information across in-person, phone, and online channels (and verify power-of-attorney for agents), refusing disclosure on failed authentication; provide policy copies within 10 days (Reg P §1016.9; NCUA §748; RFPA).

**(e) Data Accuracy & Corrections.** Propagate master-data corrections to all systems and prior recipients where applicable within 30 days for bureau-reported items, and route USPS/NCOA mismatches through Red Flags (Reg V; NCUA §748; Part 717).

**(f) Employee Access Minimization & Training.** Enforce least-privilege RBAC, quarterly access reviews, 24-hour revocation on termination, and onboarding plus annual privacy training (NCUA §748 App. A).

**(g) Third-Party Oversight & Contracts.** Require due diligence, GLBA addenda, data maps, subprocessor flow-downs, and continuous monitoring before first data transfer and on annual review (Reg P §1016.13; NCUA §748).

**(h) Secure Disposal of NPPI.** Enforce the retention schedule and certified destruction of paper, media, and data within 90 days of retention expiry (legal holds pause), retaining a certificate of destruction (16 CFR Part 682; NCUA §748).

**(i) Incident Response & Breach Notification.** Detect, contain, classify, and notify members/regulators without unreasonable delay per state clocks, file a SAR where criminal activity is suspected, and integrate privacy and legal into the IR runbook (NCUA §748 App. B; state breach laws; RFPA).

**(j) Recordkeeping, Complaints & Board Reporting.** Centralize complaint logs, notices, opt-outs, and metrics, and report to the Board at least annually and ad hoc for material incidents (NCUA §748).

**(k) Website Posting & E-SIGN Delivery.** Host the current notice in an ADA-accessible format, capture and retain e-consent artifacts, and fulfill notice copy requests within 10 days (Reg P §1016.9; 15 USC §7001).

**(l) State Variants (CA/CPRA, Vermont, Nevada) — applied as universal floor.** Separate GLBA NPPI from non-GLBA telemetry. Apply CPRA-equivalent rights (access, delete, correct, limit sensitive PI use, data minimization, purpose limitation) to all members regardless of state. Honor "Do Not Sell/Share," GPC signals, and Vermont/Nevada marketing limits for non-GLBA data.

**(m) Anonymization & Aggregation.** Permit analytics/R&D only on documented, non-identifiable data with re-identification prohibited, small-cohort thresholds applied, and methods reviewed annually.

**(n) Cookies & Online Tracking (non-GLBA).** Operate a cookie banner and preference center, block non-essential tags until consent where required, and honor universal opt-out signals for CA users.

**(o) Third-Party App/Account Connections.** Scope and consent member-authorized API connections with purpose-limited tokens and an immediate revoke path; prohibit reuse beyond consented scope.

**(p) Biometric Data for KYC.** Permit vendor face-match/liveness only under explicit contractual limits, prefer vendor-side storage, prohibit model reuse, and purge per state biometric law; offer a non-biometric path where feasible.

**(q) Children's Data.** Apply age gates, treat the service as not directed to under-13 users, and promptly delete minor data on discovery (COPPA).

Governance of these controls is centralized with the Chief Compliance Officer (with the BSA/AML Officer), with Privacy Operations, Legal, Information Security/IT, and business owners as required participants.
