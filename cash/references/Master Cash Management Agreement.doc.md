**Torrington Savings Bank Master Cash Management Agreement**  
**General Security Measures**

You agree to always use the encryption security features built into your web browser software whenever you connect to a web server utilized by Bank and/or its vendors, and to verify that each entire online session is encrypted.  

To avoid unauthorized access to the Customer’s confidential information and its accounts, you agree to adopt all reasonable security measures in using Business Online Banking, including, but not limited to, the steps listed below.

* Institute strong internal controls around Authorized Representative limits and Authorized Representative entitlement/access.  
* Check your account at least once a day and immediately report any suspicious activity to Bank.  
* Authorized Representatives shall not reveal their user IDs and Passwords to anyone. Authorized Representatives shall be instructed to use strong passwords and not to choose user IDs and Passwords based on personal information that may be determined or accessible by others such as names, birthdays, addresses, or that are similar to other existing passwords or security codes.  
* Do not keep written records of user IDs and Passwords.   
* Notify Torrington Savings Bank immediately when an Authorized Representative for any cash management services is terminated or job function changes in order to revoke access.  
* Torrington Savings Bank recommends accessing Business Online Banking via a stand-alone PC not used for any other internet-related functions.  If Customer will be using Business Online Banking PC(s) for other functions, Customer agrees to use only known commercial software and visit only known and trusted websites when browsing the internet.  
* PCs shall not be left unattended when connected to any Bank website.  
* There is a heightened risk of loss and theft of security credentials with the use of laptops and other personal devices, as well as wireless connection services.  Therefore, Bank recommends that Customer establish and maintain appropriate internal control and security practices to adequately manage the risks associated with the use of such devices and the use of wireless connection services when accessing Online Banking Services.  
* Customer shall use e-mail filtering software to screen for unsolicited email (spam). These tools can help reduce the likelihood of a virus or worm installing a malicious program on Customer’s PCs or receiving e-mail phishing attempts.  Use Web filtering software for configuration and pop-up blocking.  
* Customer shall train Authorized Representatives on the secure use of email.  Customer will use additional caution regarding unsolicited email and email from unknown senders and will use reasonable judgment before clicking on links or attachments.  
* For all Customer PCs, Customer shall keep all operating system, browser, anti-virus, firewalls, anti-spyware and other software, such as security patches, up to date.  Set these systems to automatically update on a daily basis.   
* Avoid downloading “free” software; downloads are a common source of “spyware” infections.  Customer will scan PCs with anti-virus software on a regular basis to check for new viruses or spyware.  
* After completing any activities within Business Online Banking, exit Business Online Banking using the “logoff” option.  
* After completing any activities on Online Banking, secure or erase any files that may exist in a non-encrypted state in your web services cache or in your PC’s memory to prevent unauthorized access.  
* Do not send any personal, business, financial, or account information by internet e-mail, as messages sent in this manner are sent outside areas of Business Online Banking.  
* Verify that each online session is encrypted.  
* Train employees on the system’s use and security procedures.

  The most effective loss prevention measure Customer can take is to ensure that the Customer has a strong control environment in place.  Customer must institute a robust control environment and monitor its account transactions and activities daily.  Cash management Services allows monitoring of Authorized Representative activity through use of the activity report feature of Online Banking.


  In addition, Bank recommends the use of the following Online Banking features to further ensure security of your accounts:

* Approval parameters can be set for transaction amounts for a Service, which would require approvals when the amount is less than, equal to, or greater than the specified transaction amount.    
* Limits can also be set for an Authorized Representative and by Service.  Authorized Representative limits should be set appropriately according to your established internal control policies.    
* Any templates used for ACH services should also be regularly reviewed by Customer to ensure that you are aware of each individual entry that is available for payment or collection through use of the template.
