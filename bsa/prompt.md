## 1. Purpose and Scope

Pynthia Credit Union maintains a risk-based, Board-approved integrated BSA/AML/CFT/OFAC/CIP program that meets 12 CFR § 748.2 for federally insured credit unions and applicable Treasury regulations (31 CFR Chapter X). The program verifies member identities, performs customer due diligence (including enhanced due diligence where warranted), monitors and reports suspicious activity, files required reports (CTR, SAR, CMIR, FBAR), maintains required records, screens against OFAC sanctions lists, identifies politically exposed persons, and escalates breaches. It applies to all members, accounts, transactions, channels, and third parties of the credit union. This policy consolidates the formerly separate AML/CFT, OFAC, and Customer Due Diligence programs.

## 2. Key Regulatory Authorities

- **NCUA BSA Compliance (12 CFR § 748.2)** — requires a Board-approved BSA program with internal controls, independent testing, a designated BSA Officer, training, and a CIP component (incorporating 31 CFR § 1020.220).
- **AML Program & CIP (31 CFR §§ 1020.210, 1020.220)** — AML program and customer identification program requirements.
- **Currency Transaction Reports (31 CFR §§ 1010.311, 1010.306)** — file CTRs for currency >$10,000 within 15 calendar days.
- **Suspicious Activity Reports (31 CFR § 1020.320)** — file SARs within 30 days (suspect identified) / 60 days (no suspect); continuing SARs every 90 days; SAR confidentiality.
- **Monetary Instruments Log (31 CFR § 1010.415)** — recordkeeping for $3,000–$10,000 monetary instrument purchases.
- **Funds Transfer "Travel Rule" (31 CFR § 1010.410(e),(f))** — record/transmit originator and beneficiary information for wires ≥$3,000.
- **Information Sharing (31 CFR §§ 1010.520 (314(a)), 1010.540 (314(b)))** — respond to FinCEN requests within 14 days; voluntary FI-to-FI sharing safe harbor.
- **BSA Record Retention (31 CFR § 1010.430)** — 5-year baseline retention.
- **CMIR (31 CFR § 1010.340)** — report cross-border currency >$10,000 (Form 105).
- **FBAR (31 CFR § 1010.350)** — report foreign accounts >$10,000 aggregate, due April 15 (auto-extension to October 15).
- **OFAC Sanctions (31 CFR Part 501; Part 594)** — screening, blocking/rejecting, reporting procedures and penalties; 10-year record retention effective March 12, 2025.
- **PEPs / FFIEC BSA/AML Examination Manual** — risk-based enhanced due diligence expectations for politically exposed persons (no categorical prohibition).

## 3. What This Policy Must Cover

BSA risk concentrates in identifying customers, understanding the nature and purpose of relationships, detecting and timely-reporting suspicious and reportable activity, and complying with sanctions obligations across all products, channels, and third parties. Because the credit union relies on third parties for certain AML/CFT screening, governance and independent testing of those vendors is a focus.

The policy must establish the following controls:

**(a) Governance & Delegation.** The Board establishes and administers the BSA/AML/OFAC program, designates a BSA Officer, defines roles and segregation of duties via a RACI registry, and approves the policy at least annually (interim review within 30 days of a material change).

**(b) Enterprise BSA/AML Risk Assessment.** Maintain a product/partner/channel/geography risk catalog, compute inherent-to-residual risk scores, set EDD triggers, and review every 12–18 months or on material change.

**(c) Customer Identification Program (CIP).** Enforce required identity data (legal name, DOB, address, TIN), allow documentary/non-documentary verification, block account activation until verified, and retain identity information 5 years after account closure and verification records 5 years after made.

**(d) CDD / EDD (incl. Beneficial Ownership).** Collect expected activity, source of funds/wealth, and beneficial-ownership information (natural persons owning ≥25% plus one control person); maintain high-risk playbooks; refresh per risk tier and on event-driven changes.

**(e) OFAC Screening & Holds.** Screen members, counterparties, and payments at onboarding and pre-execution; block or reject per program; file required reports under Part 501; retain OFAC records 10 years (effective March 12, 2025); apply the 50% rule and manage licenses and false positives.

**(f) Transaction Monitoring & Case Management.** Generate alerts from rules/models, triage within 2 business days, manage cases, and decide SAR/no-SAR within 30 days of detection where warranted.

**(g) CTR Filing.** Auto-aggregate cash in/out per person per business day, manage Phase I/II exemptions (DEP list), e-file CTRs within 15 days, conduct annual exemption reviews, and file FinCEN Form 110 (Designation of Exempt Person) for each exempted entity with annual renewal.

**(h) SAR Filing & Confidentiality.** Manage the SAR decision workflow, file within 30 days (suspect known) / 60 days (no suspect) with 90-day continuing-SAR cadence, retain SARs and supporting documents 5 years, restrict SAR visibility, provide monthly Board summaries, and decline subpoenas for SARs (notifying FinCEN/NCUA).

**(i) Monetary Instruments Logs.** Capture purchaser identity, instrument, and serials for $3,000–$10,000 purchases; consolidate to the central log by the 15th of the following month; retain 5 years.

**(j) Travel Rule (Wires ≥$3,000).** Require and store originator, beneficiary, and financial-institution identifiers, and validate before wire release.

**(k) Information Sharing (314(a)/(b)).** Intake FinCEN SISS requests, search across specified lookback windows, respond within 14 days, and maintain 314(b) certification and counterpart verification.

**(l) Record Retention.** Apply retention schedules by record type (5-year BSA baseline; 10-year OFAC), honor legal holds, and purge with audit logs.

**(m) Escalation Pathway.** Provide one-click breach/emergent-issue escalation to the BSA Officer/General Counsel, acknowledge internally within 1 business day, and produce an action plan within 5 business days, including regulator notifications where applicable.

**(n) Training.** Assign role-based curricula, complete within 30 days of hire and annually by policy anniversary, and track Board/committee training separately.

**(o) Independent Testing.** Conduct independent program testing every 12–18 months, map scope to controls, and track remediation to closure.

**(p) High-Risk Categories (MSB, Correspondent, Private Banking).** Apply category checklists, verify FinCEN MSB registration and state licenses, capture site visits, and refresh EDD at least annually.

**(q) CMIR (Cross-Border Currency).** Identify reportable shipments/receipts, file Form 105 within 15 days after receipt (or by mailing/shipping date if not accompanying a person), and store confirmations.

**(r) FBAR.** Inventory foreign accounts, calendar the April 15 deadline (auto-extension to October 15), and e-file via the BSA system.

**(s) Prepaid Access & Third Parties.** Require a vendor due-diligence package, contract clauses for data access/audit/sanctions, system program limits, and annual vendor review with real-time critical alerting into transaction monitoring.

**(t) PEP Screening & EDD.** Screen applicants, beneficial owners, and signers against PEP datasets at onboarding and refresh; route hits to EDD with elevated approval and adjusted monitoring; complete EDD before activation for high-risk PEPs.

**(u) FinCEN Special Measures & GTOs (Geographic Targeting Orders).** Maintain a documented process to receive, assess, and operationalize FinCEN special measures (USA PATRIOT Act § 311) and any Geographic Targeting Orders directed at the credit union or its sector; assign intake ownership to the BSA Officer, circulate to affected business lines within 1 business day of receipt, implement required recordkeeping or reporting within the GTO-specified deadline, and retain GTO compliance records for 5 years.

Governance of these controls is centralized with the Chief Compliance Officer (BSA Officer), with Compliance, BSA Operations, Vendor Management, Payments Operations, HR, and Internal Audit as required participants.

## 4. Out of Scope

- Information system safeguards and cyber incident response — see Information Security Policy.
- Third-party/vendor onboarding and oversight generally — see Third-Party Risk Policy.
- Member privacy and data-handling obligations — see Privacy Policy.
- General records management schedules outside BSA records — see Record Retention Policy.
- Detection of suspicious activity within payment rails operationally — see Electronic Payment Systems Policy.
- IRS/FinCEN Form 8300 (cash payments >$10,000 in a trade or business) — not applicable to Pynthia as a financial institution; CTR obligations under 31 CFR § 1010.311 govern equivalent cash reporting for the credit union.
