**INTRODUCTION**

This compliance policy, as approved by the Board of Directors of American Bank, is for the use and guidance of the compliance officer and/or other bank-designated officials with compliance authority.  This compliance program shall be reviewed at least annually by the president of the bank and Board of Directors or a committee thereof.  Changes to the policy shall be submitted to the Board of Directors, or a committee thereof, at its next regularly scheduled meeting.  It shall be the responsibility of the compliance officer to insure that the bank compliance policy is properly maintained.  Suggested changes to the policy may be submitted by the compliance officer to the president in writing at any time.  The Board of Directors, or a committee thereof, president, bank compliance officer and auditor shall evaluate the overall policy on an ongoing basis.

**STATEMENT**

It is the declared policy of American Bank ("the bank") to adhere to all requirements of state and federal laws and regulations.  The bank will place special emphasis upon complying with the provisions of the following:

1. Community Reinvestment Act (12 CFR 25\)

2. Consumer Credit Protection Act

1. Electronic Funds Transfer Act (12 CFR 205\)

2. Equal Credit Opportunity Act (12 CFR 202\)

3. Fair Credit Billing Act (15 USC 1666\)

4. Fair Credit Reporting Act (15 USC 1681\)

5. Truth-In-Lending Act (Reg Z) (12 CFR 27\)

6. Fair Debt Collections Act (15 USC 1692\)

3. Fair Housing Home Loan Data System (12 CFR 27\)

4. Flood Disaster Protection Act (12 CFR 22\)

5. Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (Reg C) (12 CFR 203\)

6. Interest on Deposits (Reg Q) (12 CFR 217, DIDC Rules, 12 CFR 1204\)

7. Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (Reg X) (24 CFR 3500\)

8. Right to Financial Privacy Act (12 USC 3401\)

9. Wisconsin Consumer Act

10. FDIC Advertisement of Membership Regulations (12 CFR 328\)

11. Fair Housing Act (42 USC 3605\) (24 CFR 110\)

12. Bank Secrecy Act (12 CFR 326\)

13. Availability of Funds and Collection of Checks (12 CFR 229\)

14. Truth in Savings Act (12 CFR 230\)

15. Privacy of Consumer Financial Information (12 CFR 332\)

16. Safeguarding Customer Information (12 CFR 332\)

17. Fact Act (12 CFR 222\)

18. S.A.F.E Act (12 CFR 365\)

It is the policy of the bank to respond to the credit needs of its community.  The Community Reinvestment Act Notice (see attached), adopted by the Board of Directors, delineates the community served and illustrates these areas through use of appropriate maps.  Also, the notice lists the specific type of credit available throughout the market areas.

The bank's preparedness to extend credit is subject to legal restrictions, prudent lending practices, availability of funds and safe and sound operations of the bank.  The bank encourages its officers and employees to become involved in local community organizations sponsoring development projects and redevelopment programs.  

The civic involvement provides an insight into the credit needs of the community, as well as an opportunity to express the bank's desire to satisfy those needs wherever possible.

It is also the bank's policy to encourage applications from all persons.  Also, it is the policy of the bank to adhere to Nondiscriminatory practices regarding any applicant with respect to any aspect of a credit transaction. Discrimination is prohibited on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, handicap, familial status or age (provided the applicant has capacity to contract), the applicant's good faith exercise of rights under the Consumer Credit Protection Act, or the fact that all or part of the applicant's income is derived from public assistance programs.

**ORGANIZATION AND ADMINISTRATION**

The compliance officer is appointed and shall report to the president.  The compliance officer is responsible for the day-to-day administrative function of this policy.  The compliance officer will be the bank's liaison with the regulatory authorities and the Board of Directors concerning consumer compliance matters.  The compliance officer will meet at least annually with the Board of Directors, or committee thereof, to discuss the bank's compliance program.

In addition, the bank has formed a consumer compliance group comprised of representatives from the following departments of the bank:

1. Lending

2. Bookkeeping

3. Auditing

4. Customer Service and/or Teller Area

These representatives have been assigned the responsibility of assisting the compliance officer in performing his duties.  Also, the representatives will be responsible for reviewing and monitoring compliance with consumer regulations in their respective areas.

**AREAS OF RESPONSIBILITY**

The compliance officer is responsible for the development and administration of the bank's consumer compliance program.  The compliance officer will establish written policies and Procedures necessary to ensure compliance with consumer laws and regulations.  Also, the compliance officer will be responsible for continuous monitoring for compliance with those laws and regulations.

The program reassigns specific responsibility for the day-to-day operations of the procedures.  This day-to-day responsibility may be assigned to representatives of the compliance group or other officers, department managers, supervisors and employees.

Any errors or omissions in the program, or any act not in compliance with the procedures set forth in the program, shall be reported to the compliance officer who shall take immediate action to correct the situation and prevent its reoccurrence in the future.

**INFORMATION UPDATING AND DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM**

As part of the continuing compliance program, a systematic procedure is established for the distribution of information regarding new consumer compliance requirements or updating existing requirements.  Incoming information regarding consumer compliance is immediately directed to the compliance officer.

When consumer laws or regulations change, the compliance officer will review bank policies and procedures to assure continued compliance.  The review will include loan and other policies, operating bulletins or manuals, intrabank memoranda, training manuals, bank procedures and bank forms.

After making an evaluation of the impact of the changed or new requirements and updating affected bank materials, the compliance officer will distribute the material to bank personnel who have policy or operational responsibilities in connection with the subject matter involved.  Where possible, the compliance officer will make sure that each of those persons has received and has become aware of the new or updated materials.

**REFERENCE LIBRARY**

The bank will maintain a reference library and assembled manuals on state and federal consumer laws and regulations.  This material is made available to bank personnel for use in understanding the regulatory requirements and processes.  Also, the bank will maintain in the library a list of various legal opinions affecting daily operations, in compliance with state and consumer laws and regulations.  The bank will also maintain a list containing the names and phone numbers of regulatory consumer specialists and bank legal counsel to contact regarding compliance matters.  The FDIC, Federal Reserve (regulations) and FFIEC websites will provide an excellent resource for daily compliance operations.  The compliance officer will also subscribe to FDIC E-Mail notifications.

**PERSONNEL TRAINING PROGRAM**

The bank's compliance program's effectiveness is primarily based on the performance of the individuals responsible for carrying out its provisions.  Therefore, one of the most important components of the program is the training of personnel.    
The purpose of the education program is to assure the bank personnel are knowledgeable of those laws and regulations applicable to his or her job.  The educational program is an ongoing activity within the organization.  It includes the following three phases:

1\.	**INITIAL** \- Training for new personnel in consumer regulation and compliance policies and procedures as applicable to the individual's job.

2\.	**REVIEW/UPDATE** \- Ongoing regular reviews and training sessions for personnel on current laws and regulations and update sessions for personnel covering new or revised laws and regulations and their implications on bank policies and practices.

3\.	**DISTRIBUTION** \- Maintain and make available to affected personnel current material regarding consumer laws and regulations.

The content of the training session will be determined by the particular needs of the various departments or group of bank personnel.

The compliance officer will attend training seminars that furnish current information or changes in laws.  The compliance officer will also subscribe to periodicals for keeping current in the area of compliance.

As new information is received, the compliance officer will inform the affected personnel of the changes so they can be implemented as soon as possible.

**CONSUMER COMPLAINTS AND INQUIRIES**

The bank will develop procedures whereby customer complaints are given prompt, courteous and fair attention. Customer complaints (oral and written) are routed to a designated person for proper handling.  These include any complaint which is received by the regulatory authorities.  In each case, the bank will investigate the nature of the complaint, resolve the situation and respond to the customer.

Complaints dealing with the bank's compliance with consumer legislation will be handled in conjunction with the consumer compliance officer.  Complaint letters coming into the bank will be maintained by the President and Vice President of Operations and they will be made available to regulatory authorities for review.

**ONGOING CONSUMER COMPLIANCE REVIEW**

In addition to the development and issuance of comprehensive policies and procedures, it is important to have established an ongoing review function.  Special attention will be placed on monitoring those provisions of the regulation where there is a greater risk of non-compliance.  An integral part of this compliance program is the systematic monitoring system for compliance and prompt corrective action taken to resolve problems as they occur and to prevent their reoccurrence in the future.

The bank will establish a continuous forms review program which will cover the examination of notes, applications, contracts and security agreements, disclosure statements and adverse action notices for compliance with regulations and laws.  Forms will be revised, as required, to comply with changes to existing, or the issuance of new regulations. Also, the bank will establish procedures to review advertisements and completed forms and applications for compliance.

In addition to the development and issuance of comprehensive policies and procedures, the following represents the bank's auditing procedures to be performed by the compliance officer and/or their designate.  The following consumer regulations will be tested and reported to the Board of Directors by the compliance officer or auditor:

**Regulation Z \- Truth-in-Lending**

1. Annual percentage rate – randomly select loans and test each for the proper annual percentage rate.

2. Review real estate loans and other irregular payment loans for proper disclosure of balloon payment.

3. Review consumer real estate loans and first mortgage non-purchase money loans for proper rescission disclosure forms.

**The Wisconsin Consumer Act**

1. Test consumer loans to insure that interest rates are in compliance with state usury statutes.

   2. Review a sampling of third party contracts prior to purchase to insure that they comply with all provisions of Truth-In-Lending in the Wisconsin Consumer Act and other applicable regulations.

   3. Verify that the following statement is placed on all copies of consumer loan documents when there is an (interlocking) transaction:  “Any holder of this consumer credit contract is subject to all claims and defenses which the debtor could assert against the seller of goods or services obtained pursuant hereto or with the proceeds hereof.  Recovery hereunder by the debtor shall not exceed amounts paid by the debtor hereunder.”

   4. Verify that delinquency charges and late charges do not exceed statutory limitations.

   5. Review security interest to insure that only permissible types of collateral are being taken.

   6. Review credit insurance sales procedures to insure that no borrowers are required to purchase the insurance as a provision of obtaining the loan.

   7. Verify that proper default cure procedures are being followed.

   8. Review collection procedures to see that only permissible procedures are being used.

**The Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act**

1. Review mortgage purchase money loans to insure that the proper information has been disclosed to the customers.

2. Verify that proper forms and homebuyers guides are available to the loan officers.

**The Fair Credit Reporting Act**

1. Review reject notifications to see that valid reasons are being used to reject applicants, to see that applicant is properly notified of the rejections, and to insure that the customer has been made aware of his rights, including the name and address of the federal agency that administers compliance laws.

2. Test applications to see that no prohibitive information is being obtained by the loan officer.

**Community Reinvestment Act**

1. Review the Community Reinvestment Act policy and suggest appropriate changes and revisions to senior management.

2. Verify that the Community Reinvestment Act notices are available to the customers.

3. Notify senior management and the Board of Directors of any Community Reinvestment Act complaints.

**Fair Credit Billing Act**

1. Audit once annually to insure that proper explanation of the Fair Credit Billing Act is being made to all customers.

2. Audit once annually, for accuracy to computing interest charges and APRs.

3. Review new check credit accounts for proper disclosure and documentation.

**Regulation E \- Electronic Funds Transfers and Automatic Transfers**		

1. Compliance and auditing of this department will be conducted by the officer in charge and reported to the compliance officer.

**Advertising**

1. Review all advertising to ensure clarity and compliance with all applicable consumer laws.

2. Review all advertising to ensure that they do not include any prohibited information as defined by Wisconsin Consumer Act, Regulation B, and Regulation Z.

3. All ads promoting deposits should be reviewed to see that the proper interest rates, yields and penalties are disclosed.

4. All ads shall include the FDIC and Equal Housing Logos as required.

5. The bank shall post proper notification of all anti-discrimination policies as appropriate.

**Interest on Deposits**

1. Review Now accounts for eligibility.

2. Review Money Market account transactions.

**Bank Secrecy Act**

1. Review filing of Currency Transaction Reports and Suspicious Activity Reports.

2. Review exemptions

3. Review the Customer Identification Program.

**Reg CC – Availability of Funds and Collection of Checks**

1. Review Funds Hold Notices.

2. Review changes to the Funds Availability Disclosure.

**Reg DD – Truth in Savings**

1. Review rate sheets for the proper disclosures and calculations of APY.

**Privacy**

1. Review the Privacy notice

2. Determine disclosure to new customers and required mailing to existing customers.

**Safeguarding Customer Information**

1. Review Customer Information Security Program

2. Review risk assessment.

**FACT Act**

1. Review procedures for alerts from credit reporting agencies (credit reports).

2. Review required credit score disclosures for mortgage loan applicants.

3. Determine whether the consumer received notice prior to or within 30 days after reporting negative information to credit reporting agencies

**S.A.F.E Act**

1. Review procedures for required registration of mortgage loan originators (MLO’s)

**PROGRAM REVIEW**

In addition to the ongoing review, an internal audit will periodically review the adequacy of the compliance program and the level of the bank's adherence to these policies and procedures.  The compliance officer will participate in the audit.  He will either directly, or through the auditor, report the findings of the audit to the Board of Directors.

The compliance officer will act as the liaison between the bank and the regulatory authority regarding the bank's consumer examination.  The compliance officer will maintain close contact with the examiners in order to assure that problem areas raised in the regulatory consumer compliance examination are satisfactorily resolved.
