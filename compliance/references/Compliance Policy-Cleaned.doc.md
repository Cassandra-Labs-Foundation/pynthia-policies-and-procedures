# **COMPLIANCE POLICY**

**Commitment**

 Cornerstone Bank is committed to maintaining a high level of compliance with all consumer laws and regulations.  The Bank considers compliance a high priority.  The Board of Directors and Management will provide the Compliance and Risk Management Committee and its members with the time, resources and complete support to effectuate and implement this compliance program.  The Bank believes that keeping its customers informed and treating each person justly is good business.  We will strive to achieve a high level of compliance with all applicable federal and state regulatory requirements, acknowledging that non-compliance can expose the Bank to substantial risks and penalties.

**Goals**

We will strive to serve our customers better by providing consumer protection for all.  The Bank practices fair lending adherence such that no one will be discriminated against or discouraged from submitting an application due to race, color, national origin, religion, sex, handicap, familial status, age, marital status, receipt of public assistance, the good faith to exercise of one's rights under the Consumer Credit Protection Act, ancestry or sexual orientation.  Bank policies and procedures are written and implemented to comply with all aspects of federal and state compliance requirements and statutes.  When changes or developments to compliance requirements occur, we will respond timely to accommodate any legal changes and to implement new procedures to address the issues.

**COMPLIANCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM**

**Management Structure and Reporting**

	The Board of Directors are ultimately responsible for the Compliance Program. The Board empowers Management to implement an effective Compliance Program and garners the unequivocal support of all employees to adhere to the Program. Furthermore, the Board is responsible for providing adequate resources to the VP-Compliance, BSA & Security and Compliance staff in order to support training and program implementation. The involvement and support of the Board and Management are critical to the implementation of an effective Compliance Program. The Board will approve the Compliance Program at least annually.

In order to execute our comprehensive, written compliance program, the Board of Directors shall appoint a Compliance Officer.  The VP-Compliance, BSA & Security serves in that capacity. A Compliance and Risk Management Committee, under the direction of the VP-Compliance, Security & BSA, shall have the responsibility to oversee the entire compliance function of the institution.  The VP- Compliance, BSA & Security shall have the authority to delegate specific tasks to be performed by other qualified individuals, but shall retain control over the function as a whole.  The Board of Directors pledge their support to the Compliance and Risk Management Committee and its members to help effectuate any necessary procedures and changes concerning compliance.

While the Compliance and Risk Management Committee is responsible for  the Bank's overall compliance posture, each member of the Compliance and Risk Management Committee is accountable for compliance issues in his/her respective area or department.  The VP-Compliance, Security & BSA, with assistance from the AVP-Compliance Officer, will be responsible for coordinating and overseeing compliance activities for the Compliance and Risk Management Committee. The minutes of the Committee will be presented to the Audit Committee on a quarterly basis and are made available for review by the full Board of Directors by posting to Director’s Access.

**Risk Assessment**

The Bank has developed a compliance risk assessment. Compliance risk is the risk to earnings or capital or the Bank’s reputation arising from violations or non-conformance with laws, rules, and regulations, prescribed practices or ethical standards. Compliance risk also arises in situations where the laws or rules governing certain Bank products or activities of the Bank’s clients may be ambiguous or untested. This risk exposes the institution to fines, civil money penalties, payment of damages and the voiding of contracts. Compliance risk can lead to a diminished reputation and limited business opportunities. The Bank utilizes this risk assessment to identify areas where there may be increased compliance risk to the Bank and may warrant increased monitoring of such areas. The risk assessment will be reviewed at least annually by the Compliance and Risk Management Committee and the Bank’s high risk areas will be highlighted and monitoring will be increased in these areas.

**Responsibilities**

*VP-Compliance, Security & BSA and Compliance Department*

The VP-Compliance, Security & BSA will have the following duties:

* Assist in the development of compliance policies and procedures  
* Assist and support the Bank’s various departments by providing guidance and assistance in all aspects of the Bank’s Compliance Program.  
* Work with the  VP-Learning & Development, to train management and employees on consumer protection laws and regulations  
* Review policies and procedures for compliance with applicable laws and regulations and the Bank’s stated policies and procedures  
* Assess emerging issues and potential liabilities  
* Develop and monitor the Compliance Risk Assessment  
* Manage consumer complaint process and response  
* Periodically report compliance activities and audit/review to the Board or an appropriate committee thereof  
* Assist auditors and examiners during audits and regulatory exams  
* Ensure corrective action to any audit or exam findings  
* Establish and maintain records retention policies to ensure compliance with legal requirements.

*Compliance and Risk Management Committee*

The Compliance and Risk Management Committee, through the efforts of its members, is responsible for establishing, implementing, maintaining and monitoring the Bank's compliance with all applicable federal and state consumer compliance requirements.  The committee is governed by a charter (Exhibit A). 

*Employees*

Every Bank employee is affected by consumer protection statutes and regulations.  As such, each employee will be responsible for assuring that his/her own work complies with all applicable requirements.  All employees will understand all applicable compliance requirements commensurate with their responsibilities and job functions.  As part of each employee's annual performance review, consideration will be given to the employee's work and effort that is made to see to it that his/her performance complies with regulatory requirements, Bank policies and procedures and the compliance program.

**Components of the Compliance Management System**

1\.  Education:  Compliance staff and Bank employees with compliance responsibilities will be familiar with all of the compliance rules and regulations that affect the Bank and will attend training seminars to further enhance their knowledge of applicable requirements.  The Compliance and Risk Management Committee, through the VP-Compliance, Security & BSA will monitor changes and developments in laws, regulations, and examination procedures.  To accomplish this task, the Compliance and Risk Management Committee will utilize resource materials, regulatory bulletins and trade publications.

2\.  Training:  Compliance training is an ongoing function, with the major purpose being to ensure all employees, officers and board members know the requirements applicable to their responsibilities.  The Compliance and Risk Management Committee will oversee compliance training, working in conjunction with the  VP-Learning & Development and will be conducted during the orientation process for applicable new hires and individuals attaining new responsibilities.  All affected employees, officers and board members will receive additional training in response to statutory requirements, changes or amendments in the regulation, or if non-compliance with a compliance requirement is discovered.  Training may consist of a review of policies and procedures, videos, on-line modules, applicable documents and disclosures, and class lectures.

3\.  Policies and Procedures:  The Compliance and Risk Management Committee will appoint individuals to periodically review the Bank's written policies and procedures to ensure they are in compliance with applicable compliance regulations.    The Compliance and Risk Management Committee will amend the policies and procedures to reflect any changes or amendments concerning the statutory requirements. 

4\.  Forms and Website Review:  The Compliance Department will review and update the Bank's disclosures and documents to ensure they are in compliance with applicable compliance regulations.  The Compliance Department will revise the disclosures and documents to reflect any changes or amendments concerning the statutory requirements. Additionally the Compliance Department will review and approve any changes to the website or any new pages or items added to the website or social media.

5\.  Product Development and Modification: The Compliance Program requires a compliance review be conducted when a new product is developed or an existing product is modified or a new service is introduced or a current one modified. This is accomplished by the VP-Compliance, Security & BSA being a member of the Products and Services Committee. 

6\.  Consumer Complaints:  The VP-Compliance, BSA & Security will establish written procedures concerning consumer complaints. Please see the Bank’s [Complaint Resolution Policy](F:\\\\Policies\\\\Complaint%20%20Resolution%20Policy.doc).  The complaints may allege a violation of the consumer's rights or may allege a deficiency concerning the Bank's operations.  The VP-Compliance, BSA & Security shall oversee the handling of the complaint from its initial stages to its final resolution and shall report consumer complaints to the Compliance and Risk Management Committee and the Audit Committee.

7\.  Monitoring Program:  The Compliance and Risk Management Committee, through the VP-Compliance, Security & BSA and the AVP-Compliance Officer, will be the liaison with the independent audit firm concerning compliance testing and reporting.  The results of the independent testing will be presented to the Compliance and Risk Management Committee and the Audit Committee.  The Compliance and Risk Management Committee will be responsible for following up on any necessary corrective measures to ensure that action has been taken and the action addresses the violation noted. In house monitoring is conducted by the Compliance Department on the Bank’s higher risk areas and results are reported to the Compliance and Risk Management Committee and the Audit Committee. The frequency is based on the Bank’s current risk level. Any areas not monitored internally may be reviewed by the Bank’s external compliance auditor. 

8\. Advertising:  AVP-Compliance Officer or designee reviews all Bank advertising prior to publication to ensure it meets regulatory requirements.

**Audit/Compliance Testing**

A qualified, independent firm will conduct an audit and review of the Bank's compliance with established regulations. The frequency is determined by the risk assessment prepared by the audit firm. The responsibility of the firm will be to conduct formal, periodic reviews of our compliance with all applicable federal and state laws and regulations.  The firm shall prepare written reports that will be presented to the VP-Compliance, Security & BSA, Management and the Audit Committee.  The reports shall make note of all violations, weaknesses and potential deficiencies.  Concentration on problem areas will be emphasized.  

The Bank also utilizes an audit tracking matrix to track audit findings, exam findings, internal monitoring findings and management’s responses and establishes timelines for items to be addressed. This matrix is reviewed by the Compliance and Risk Management Committee and the Audit Committee on a quarterly basis.  The Board of Directors will approve this policy on an annual basis or more frequently when changes are deemed necessary.

**COMPLIANCE AND RISK MANAGEMENT COMMITTEE CHARTER**  
**Purpose**

The Compliance & Risk Management Committee has the general responsibility to oversee the Banks’ compliance, risk and ethics programs, policies and procedures. The purpose of the Committee is to: (i) oversee the Banks’ implementation of compliance programs, policies and procedures that are designed to respond to the various compliance, regulatory and other risks facing the bank; (ii) assist the Board of Directors in fulfilling its oversight responsibility for the Compliance Management System  and Risk Management standards set by the governing regulatory agencies; and (iii) perform other compliance and risk duties as directed by the Board of Directors or Audit Committee of the Bank (iv) Manage the Bank’s Enterprise Risk Management Risk Assessment.

**Membership, Structure and Organization**

***Members***\-Refer to the next page for a list of members and their titles

 ***Meetings***\-The Committee shall meet as frequently as it deems appropriate, but not less than four times per year. Currently meetings are scheduled for the second or third Wednesday of each month during the months of March, June, September and December, at 10:30 am or following the monthly board meeting, to take place in the Southbridge boardroom.

 ***Delegation of Authority***\-The Committee may establish subcommittees consisting of one or more members or other directors or management to carry out such duties as the Committee may delegate.

 ***Charter*****\-**The Committee shall annually review the adequacy of this charter and recommend any proposed changes to the Board for approval. 

 ***Agenda & Minutes***\-the Chairperson of the Committee shall be responsible for setting the agenda for the meetings and the agenda shall be sent prior to each meeting to allow meaningful review. Minutes will be taken at each meeting, which will be sent to the Audit Committee and ultimately the Board of Directors of the Bank.  
                 
**Responsibilities- The responsibilities of the Committee shall include**

***Compliance and Risk Matters***  
The Committee shall have oversight responsibility for matters including the overall compliance management system, overall risk management, legal or regulatory exposure and material reports or inquiries from regulatory agencies. This includes current compliance and risk issues, changes to existing or new regulations/legislation and risk related regulations or requirements, any examinations that are conducted, and the tracking of items on the audit tracking matrix to ensure timely resolution.

***Compliance and Risk Programs, Policies and Procedure***  
The Committee shall oversee the Banks’ compliance and risk efforts with respect to                                relevant policies, procedures, laws and regulations and risk management processes. Ensure there are appropriate monitoring systems in place to identify, log, and report any violations of laws, rules and regulations and other instances of non-compliance.

***Complaint Management***  
The Committee will receive a summary of each complaint that is logged that required management response. The VP-Compliance, BSA & Security monitors the complaint log of the Bank to determine if there is a common pattern or theme to the complaints or evidence that there may be a disclosure or training issue.

***Risk Assessments***  
The Committee shall review compliance risk assessments annually. 

***Training***  
Oversees the implementation of a training program which assures that appropriate staff maintains a satisfactory level of knowledge, laws, rules and regulations.
