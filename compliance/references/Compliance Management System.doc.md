The Board of Directors, Executive Management, and employees of “Bank”  are committed to serving the public on a fair and nondiscriminatory basis and to being a major force in the development of the bank’s local communities.

To achieve this it is necessary that the bank be in compliance with all applicable laws, statutes, regulations, and interpretations. The Board of Directors recognizes that a successful Compliance Management System (CMS) will contain the following elements to Prevent, Detect, and Correct compliance issues:

* Board of Directors oversight and Executive Management involvement  
* Compliance Officer to oversee and administer an effective CMS  
* Risk Assessments  
* Policies, Procedures, and Internal Controls  
* Monitoring and independent Audit function  
* Training of bank personnel  
* Consumer Complaint Management  

The Board has appointed ??? as Compliance Officer to supervise the CMS. If the Compliance Officer changes or cannot perform their duties, the CFO and/or Operations Officer will fill in until a new Compliance Officer is appointed. The Compliance Officer will attend seminars, workshops, and conferences to maintain and expand compliance knowledge and responsibilities as needed.

The Compliance Officer is responsible for overall bank compliance including:

* Maintaining knowledge of applicable compliance laws, statutes, regulations, and interpretations  
* Conducting risk assessments  
* Formulating and updating bank policies, procedures, and internal controls  
* Coordinating the training of bank personnel with Human Resources   
* Monitoring consumer complaints   
* Coordinating visits and reviews with outside consultants and auditors  
* Monitoring exams with regulators

The Compliance Officer is responsible for the oversight and implementation of the bank’s compliance efforts. This includes the authority to call and establish written agendas for meetings (ex. Loan and Deposit committee meetings), delegate responsibilities to other departments, conduct training and devote sufficient bank time and resources necessary to maintain compliance. Outside consultants will conduct periodic reviews including transaction testing and will also be a contact for interpretation and assistance as needed. The Compliance Officer will report to the Board of Directors at least quarterly to review any possible regulation changes, submit policies for approval, document training sessions, and provide findings and recommendations received from audits and exams including responses and any corrective actions.

Department managers and supervisors will assist the Compliance Officer and will be responsible for overseeing compliance in their functional areas. This responsibility includes:

•	Knowledge of the laws and regulations affecting their area	  
•	Assist in the drafting and implementation of policies, procedures, and internal controls  
•	Coordinating and monitoring any corrections and changes

The Compliance Officer will coordinate with department supervisors and Human Resources the training of new and existing employees on regulations to ensure compliance. Regulations that require annual training are addressed at the annual “Team Development Day” where employees from all branches are encouraged to attend. Online Education courses may also be used. New employees are introduced to compliance regulations and policies during the Onboarding process.

Correspondence from regulatory agencies and other resources regarding compliance should be directed to the Compliance Officer. The Compliance Officer will consult with department managers and supervisors to review and analyze the correspondence to determine if any action or distribution will follow. Changes required will be implemented including new procedures being developed and training sessions scheduled for affected bank personnel. Attendance records of training sessions will be submitted to and retained by the Compliance Officer and Human Resources. If it is determined training sessions are not necessary, a memorandum concerning new procedures may be circulated to appropriate personnel.

The Compliance Officer will coordinate with Executive Management on how to monitor and manage changes effectively in laws, regulations, products, and services. The bank will deal with a multitude of expected, strategic, and unexpected changes. Change is a process, not a one-time event. Therefore, the awareness, desire, knowledge, ability, and reinforcement of all employees and the Board of Directors are needed for a successful Compliance Management System.
