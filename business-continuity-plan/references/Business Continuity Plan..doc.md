**PURPOSE AND OBJECTIVE**

A disaster is any event resulting in damage to equipment, damage to physical location, and /or injury to personnel, to the extent of which is serious enough to require action for recovery, and is outside the authority of those initially aware of such an event.  If the scenario described above occurs, the Business Continuation Plan will be put into effect by the Recovery Team and the situation will be declared as a "disaster".

This plan addresses the course of action that will be taken in the event the organization experiences a disaster as described above.

**Plan Objective:**

**PROTECTION OF PERSONNEL:**  
The first objective to the Business Continuation Plan will be to provide for the protection of personnel during and after a disaster situation.

**PROTECTION OF ASSETS AND RECORDS:**  
The second objective will be the protection of assets, records, documents, and vital statistical date, and the preservation and safekeeping of our customers' property.

**CONTINUITY OF MANAGEMENT:**  
In a disaster, members of the Board of Directors, key executives, or vital staff members could be killed or incapacitated.  This plan will provide for the continuity of management and personnel requirements.  It will also define the lines of authority and responsibility during and after a disaster.

**MINIMIZE LOSSES AND RECOVERY TIME:**  
In some disasters, a "business as usual" environment will not always be possible.  Time, money, and opportunities will be lost; thus, another objective will be to recover the critical business functions at an alternative location within 24 hours.

**RESUMPTION OF NORMAL OPERATIONS:**  
Our reputation and the public's confidence in the stability of the bank are important resources, and a lengthy interruption in service could erode or destroy that confidence and reputation.  The final objective of the Business Continuation Plan, will be to resume operational status of the critical business functions within 72 hours and to maintain operational status at the alternate locations until normal support and operational capabilities can be re-established in a permanent environment.

**BUSINESS CONTINUATION PROCESS**

First Alert:

Any employee discovering a potential disaster situation will notify the Management Recovery Team Leader (CEO) or the Facilities Manager or Security Officer and advise him/her of the situation.

If an initial assessment of the nature and impact of the problem indicates a possible disaster situation, the alerted individual will notify the Management Recovery Team Members.

The Management Recovery Team Leader will assign one or more of it's members to assess the damaged site and report back to the Management Recovery Team Leader and inform him/her if the situation appears serious enough to consider a disaster declaration.

Disaster Declaration:

\_\_\_		The individual assigned to assess the damage will proceed to the damaged facility immediately, and provide input to the Management Recovery Team on the state of the facility.

\_\_\_\_	The available Management Recovery Team members will meet to assess the possible disaster situation, and to categorize the situation as "Return to Business" or "Disaster Declaration" as soon as possible.

	\_\_\_\_  If a "Return to Business" is declared, the building will be opened for normal business and normal operations will resume.  
	\_\_\_\_	If a "Disaster Declaration"...   
			\_\_\_\_ 	The Management Recovery Team will arrange for a pre-recorded message to this effect to be placed on the main number of the affected location and will notify the Recovery Team Leaders of the "Disaster Declaration".  
			\_\_\_\_	Each Recovery Team Leader will try each team member once.  Notifying each person of the declaration and then proceeding to the designated alternate facility.  Once there, they will attempt again any incomplete calls.

**Maintaining The Recovery Plan**

Purpose:

Due to the constantly changing business environment, it is essential to set up methods of maintaining the contingency plan to ensure its viability.  Maintenance of the Continuation plan should be done at least annually.  Each department will be responsible for submitting changes as they occur.  Annual review should be done by each area to ensure the accuracy of the plan. 

Team responsibilities should be reviewed annually.  In addition The Off-Site Back up of Critical Data should be reviewed to ensure items are being stored off-site as listed.  

Testing:

Review accuracy of Plan:   Annually a review should be done to evaluate the plan adequacy.  This approach should evaluate the timeliness of plan distribution, the disaster team member  list,  contact lists, employee lists and team responsibilities.  Verification that the critical data backups are being stored off-site should be completed.  In addition, a review should be done to assess the alternate facilities for adequacy.  The management team and team leaders need to look at the their unit and explore how they would return to normal operation if the entire bank were destroyed.  Using this worst case scenario test it will allow for a complete review of the contingency plan. 

Selective testing should be used to verify that data processing and computer operations will be operational at the alternate location.  This type of test can be done without interrupting normal business operations.  The test includes loading the most critical applications on the alternate equipment to verify operational status at the alternate facility.  

Test evaluation process is helpful in identifying the areas within the recovery plan that need modification.  The management team members input is vital in determining where the plan needs modification and improvement.
