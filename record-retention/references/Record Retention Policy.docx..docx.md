

**RECORD RETENTION AND DESTRUCTION POLICY**

**Approved 09/18/2014**

**SUBJECT:     RECORD RETENTION AND DESTRUCTION POLICY**

## 

**PURPOSE:**

## To provide a schedule of minimum records to be retained by the bank and used as a guide for creating, implementing, and maintaining a comprehensive record retention program consistent with the bank’s specific goals, objectives and strategies.  

## 

## The bank will maintain records in accordance with state and federal recordkeeping requirements.  Specifically, in accordance with 5-2A-9, of the Alabama Banking Code, a “Record Retention Regulation” which outlines minimum supervisory-related records and retention periods in accordance with 5-4A-1, Code of Alabama, 1975\.  These minimum requirements do not supplant any other record requirements such as but not limited to GAAP, ACH Network rules, and NASD.  The bank will maintain policy and procedures to comply with additional requirements of these governing bodies.

The bank must comply with Interagency Guidelines Establishing Information Security Standards (12 CFR 364, Appl. B) which sets guidelines and standards for administrative, technical and physical safeguards to protect security, confidentiality, and integrity of customer information (Part 364-Safety & Soundness Standards) in relation with the retention and destruction of bank records.  

This policy provides for systematic, consistent disposal of obsolete and unnecessary records under the continuing authority from the Board of Directors; this policy should help prevent overcrowding of files and improve efficiency, as unnecessary records hamper locating of essential material and increase cost of storing records; and this policy should also ensure that documents are promptly provided to authorities in the course of legal investigations or lawsuits.

**SCOPE:**

This policy applies to all bank directors, officers and employees. 

**EFFECTIVE DATE:**  

This policy and the procedures incorporated herein by reference and as may be amended, are effective upon approval by the Board of Directors of the Bank.

**RESPONSIBILITY AND ADMINISTRATION:**

The bank’s SVP of Operations and Finance is responsible for monitoring bank record retention and destruction activity and reporting to the Board of Directors and for monitoring and updating this policy and for assuring that the bank’s records are maintained and destroyed according to Schedule A – Retention Items. 

Annual review of this policy will be performed with necessary revisions to policy and record retention schedule being made after consideration of the following factors:  changes in state and Federal reporting requirements; changes in institution services and/or products; changes in overall complexity; changes in consumer compliance requirements; technological advances; and changes in storage costs. 

Each department will designate an employee as that department’s records retention contact.  The department contact will be responsible for the proper completion of retention.   

Training responsibilities will be assigned to the Operations department and the manager of each identified division or their designee.  
 

**REASONS FOR RECORD RETENTION:**

**Compliance:**	Certain laws and regulations require banks to retain records as evidence that the bank has complied with those laws and regulations.  
**Legal:**	There are statutes of limitation periods which are timeframes that claims can be brought against the bank.  
**Administration:**	The bank needs to retain certain records to handle day-to-day banking operations with its customers.  
**Managerial:**	Like a regular business corporation, the bank needs to retain certain records to handle its internal business operations.

Record Retention requirements are not limited to just one regulatory agency.  Certain documents are governed by federal law and certain documents are governed by state law.  

Not all documents are required to be retained.  Required retention periods can be triggered by different event such as stating dates, date an account is closed and date a disclosure is given.

**RECORD RETETION METHODS:**  
Records may be retained in various forms, including but not limited to, hard copies; photocopies; computer printouts; microfilm; microfiche; imaging; or other types of electronic media storage that can be readily reproduced into paper copies.  

Electronic records must be accurate, accessible and of such integrity to enable auditing authorities to determine a financial institution’s financial condition and the substance and purpose of all transactions.  

Records that are not listed in the policy, but are substantially similar to those listed in the Schedule A \-Retention Items, must be retained for the appropriate period of time.

**DOCUMENT DESTRUCTION:**  
All document destruction will be in accordance with Interagency Guidelines Establishing Information Security Standards (12 CFR 364, Appl. B).  A licensed Document Destruction vendor should be utilized to destroy all records determined requiring such destruction by the bank’s Information Security Program.  

**TRAINING:**  
Training will be provided to all bank employees on an annual basis.  Departmental training will be developed by each department’s designated record retention and destruction associate and will assure the department associates are trained on this policy and the department’s record retention requirements.

**DEFINITIONS:**

Hard Copies:	A permanent reproduction, or copy, in the form of a physical object, of any media suitable for direct use by a person (in particular paper), of displayed or transmitted data.

Photocopies:	A copy of a document using a photocopier that reproduces paper documents and other visual images.

Computer Printouts:		Hard copy paper versions of reports produced using a computer program.  

Microfilm:	Films containing micro-reproductions of documents for transmission, storage, reading, and printing.  This is a microform utilizing film (reels) format.

Microfiche:	A microform utilizing flat sheets formats to contain micro-reproductions of documents for transmission, storage, reading, and printing.

Imaging: 	Creating digital images, generally by scanning, or through digital photography.  

Electronic Media Storage:  	The storage of electronic records or images on an electronic device such as a server or computer hard drive.

**RECORD RETENTION AND DESTRUCTION PROCEDURES:**  
This policy assesses and identifies the contents of each department’s or division’s documents and files, identifies the current process of record retention of each item, and documents the proper retention for documents within the areas of responsibility.

Each department’s designated record retention contact will review their area’s retention items as documented in Schedule A – Retention Items at least annually for updating to current regulatory requirements.  Any updates to policy should be forwarded to the SVP – Operations and Finance, for policy changes and policy review and approval by the Board of Directors.

Exhibit 1 – Destruction Log, made a part of this policy, should be used by each department to log all documents being retained.  These forms should be maintained by each designated record retention contact for review by internal and external examiners.  

Retained documents should be placed in Banker Boxes or similar style boxes with the following minimum information noted on the front of the box:  Box Number, Content Description, From and To Dates, and Date to be Destroyed (Destruction Date).  This information should correspond with the information noted on the Destruction Log show in Exhibit 1\.

At a reasonable time after the documented destruction date for retained records, the records should be placed in a destruction bin or delivered to, an approved document destruction vendor for destruction.  The document destruction should be documented on the department’s log by two verifying associates initialing the log and by attaching any destruction certificate received from the document destruction vendor.  
   
FIS, our core processor, maintains a large portion of our critical file retention concerning customer and bank records by use of electronic record archives. It is the responsibility of each department to assure the proper retention timeframes are in place for the records maintained on the archive.  

Email record archives are the responsibility of the Information Technology department.  Proper retention timeframes for email should be tested periodically to assure archived emails are retrievable.
