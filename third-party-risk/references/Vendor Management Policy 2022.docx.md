**American Community Bank & Trust**  
**Vendor Management Policy**

**Statement of Need**  
This policy provides a framework to identify, monitor, assess and report risks to the bank that result from entering relationships with third-party vendors (hereinafter “vendor(s)”.

**Purpose**  
The board of directors, to provide authority to executive management, to oversee and establish specific criteria in establishing and maintaining vendor relationships.

**Scope**   
American Community Bank & Trust relies on vendors to provide goods and services to the bank.    These vendors range from mission-critical and other critically essential functions to routine vendors the bank relies on for daily business operations.    The use of vendors allows the bank provide products and solutions to clients to remain competitive in the industry.   It is the policy of American Community Bank & Trust to exercise appropriate due diligence over vendors and ensure vendors have policies and procedures in place to comply with regulations and laws to cover their products and services.  The AVP of Information Systems will be responsible for the vendor due diligence and oversight of the vendor program.

Bank by-laws identify individuals who have the authorization to execute contracts on behalf of the bank.  Depending on the scope and complexity of the vendor relationship, a vendor contract may need to be reviewed by external counsel.  

**Vendor Selection and Contracts**  
The vendor or service provider process begins when management decides to implement a new service or offering.    A request for proposal (RFP) may be used depending on the service and level or risk.

Vendor and Contract Considerations, if applicable, should include:

* Existence and corporate history  
* Qualifications, backgrounds and reputation of company and principals  
* Cost/Compensation  
* Performance Standards  
* Regulatory Compliance  
* Reports  
* Audit  
* Confidentiality and Security  
* Business Resumption and Contingency Plans  
* Default and Termination  
* Vendor Management of their third-party vendors

Vendor contracts, if applicable, should clearly set forth the rights and responsibilities of each party to the contract, including the following:

* Time frame covered by the contract  
* Specifications of the service or product to be provided  
* Software support and maintenance  
* Service Level Agreements (if applicable)  
* Training  
* Requirement for the third-party to comply with all applicable laws, regulations and regulatory guidance including but not limited to GLBA security and privacy standards  
* Authorization for the institution to review the third party for compliance

**Vendor Relationship Management Ownership**  
Vendor relationships require periodic management and maintenance.    A vendor relationship owner will be identified and have the assignment of management responsibility for the vendor relationship.

**Risk Assessment**  
The risk assessment is utilized to determine the degree of oversight required to manage the vendor relationship.

* Low Inherent Risk vendors will be subject to a review every three years with updates to the risk assessment and due diligence documentations available.  
    
* Medium Inherent Risk vendors will be reviewed bi-annually will be reviewed bi-annually and are required to have a risk assessment performed every two years with updated to the Vendor Risk Assessment and all due diligence documents.    
    
* High Inherent Risk vendors will be reviewed annually with updates to the Vendor Risk Assessment.  The bank will obtain due diligence documentation for file.  The vendor will be monitored for adverse news, deteriorating financial condition, and regulatory events.

**Risks**  
Inherent Risk  
There is an inherent risk when contracting with a third-party for operations or services.  The inherent risk of the relationship is increased with a vendor when:

1. The vendor performs mission-critical functions (i.e. core processing) or processes transactions for the bank.  
2. The vendor maintains an external connection to the bank network for monitoring or support purposes  
3. The vendor stores or has access to non-public personal information of bank customers or employees as defined by the Graham Leach Bliley Act (GLBA).  
4. The vendor performs services on bank premises on bank equipment  
5. The vendor interacts with bank customers in a servicing capacity  
6. The vendor stores sensitive data in the cloud.  Cloud Storage is defined as the vendor provides a service model in which bank data is maintained, stored, managed, backed up and made available to bankers via the internet.

Residual Risk  
The residual risk in a vendor relationship is the level of risk that remains after all due diligence, controls and mitigating actions have been considered or put in place.

**Risk of using vendors**

* Compliance Risks – violations of laws, rules, or regulations, UDAP  
* Reputation – dissatisfied customers, violations of law  
* Operational Risk – disruption of normal business and/or loss to the bank due to absence of the vendor’s service or product or from failed processes or systems, loss of data that results in privacy issues.   Would require proactive planning and management of more than 180 days for replacement vendor  
* Transaction Risk – problems with service delivery  
* Financial – inability of vendor to meet its contractual obligations

**Vendor Relationship Termination**  
The bank maintains a Master Contract List, along with the anticipated expiration of the vendor contract.  Vendor relationships are revisited prior to renewal to determine if management intends to renew relationship or would prefer to terminate and exit the vendor.

**Vendor Review and Reporting**  
The bank will complete the vendor risk analysis, due diligence reviews and maintain vendor files.  An annual status report of all, or at least High and Medium vendors, will be provided to the bank’s enterprise risk committee and board of directors.

**Regulatory Reporting**  
The bank will comply with Section 7(c)(2) of the Bank Service Company Act states that any FDIC-supervised institution that has services performed by a third party "shall notify such agency of the existence of the service relationship within 30 days after the making of such service contract or the performance of the service, whichever occurs first." As defined in Section 3 of the Act, these services include "check and deposit sorting and posting, computation and posting of interest and other credits and charges, preparation and mailing of checks, statements, notices, and similar items, or any other clerical, bookkeeping, accounting, statistical, or similar functions performed for a depository institution."
